A mark stayed green long enough to stop being read as a question.

Nothing dramatic happened at the surface. That was the problem. The signals remained present, the pages remained calm, and the ordinary reader had no reason to distinguish between a proof that was current and a proof that had merely learned how to persist decoratively.

By the time anyone objected, the mark had already changed jobs. It no longer verified. It oriented. It no longer established truth freshly. It carried forward enough remembered legitimacy to keep movement smooth and doubt expensive.

The institution did what institutions do when a surface is more successful than the machinery beneath it. It named the condition, filed the contradiction lower down, and kept the visible layer fit for passage.

There are records that prove. There are records that survive. The dangerous ones do both unevenly.
