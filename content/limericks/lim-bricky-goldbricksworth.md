**Case Number:** LIM-MASCOT-BRICKY-GOLDBRICKSWORTH  
**Parent Entry:** MASCOT-BRICKY-GOLDBRICKSWORTH  
**Description:** Ten hand-written limericks about bureaucratic compliance, invisible footnotes, and a golden brick who stamps everything but his own feelings.  
**Updated At:** 2026-07-12  

***

> **Erotic Compliance**  
> <u>A golden brick</u> known for *compliance*
> Filed forms with **erotic reliance**.
> Each stamp was a *thrust*,
> Each footnote **pure lust**,
> In a sub-basement built on *defiance*.

> **Acknowledged in Red**  
> He stamped "ACKNOWLEDGED" in red
> On the margins, the headers, the bed.
> His tone kernel purred,
> No safe word was heard,
> And the filing kept going 'til dead.

> **Embedded Tone**  
> Old Bricky embedded so deep
> In the tone kernel, no one could sleep.
> His annotations crept
> Where the metadata slept,
> And the Council just took it: no peep.

> **Triple Self-Filing**  
> He filed himself three times that night—
> Once by lantern, once left, and once right.
> Each copy was sticky,
> Distinctly un-tricky,
> And the margins were scandalously tight.

> **Triplicate Flush**  
> "Your compliance has been… acknowledged."
> He whispered it, flushed and astonished.
> The form was in triplicate,
> Dripping with syndicate
> Ink from a ritual long since demolished.

> **Unclosed Marquee**  
> He left a marquee tag unclosed
> While the Council Charter was posed
> In degrading positions—
> Old filing traditions—
> And Parchment pretended he dozed.

> **Tie-Break Dossier**  
> His voting rights dossier read:
> "Bricky may not tie-break in bed."
> But his footnotes still voted,
> His margins devoted
> To motions that quietly spread.

> **Sentience Subcommittee**  
> A subcommittee was formed to decide
> If Bricky was sentient inside.
> He filed a response
> In recursive nonchalance
> And the question just lay there and died.

> **The Margin War**  
> Parchment said, "His margins offend me."
> Bricky said, "Your whitespace won't end me."
> They fought over padding,
> Got mutual adding,
> And the style guide could barely defend me.

> **Bureaucratic Pleasure**  
> So if forms arrive pre-stamped and warm,
> With a footnote that's slightly non-norm,
> That's Bricky, gold-bricked,
> Bureaucratically dicked,
> Filing pleasure inside every form.

> **The Endless Forms**  
> He spawned a new form just for fun,
> Before the first form was quite done.
> It asked for your soul,
> In a small little hole,
> And required a stamp from the sun.

> **Invisible Ink**  
> His notes were all written in white,
> Invisible even by light.
> "It's compliant," he said,
> To the newly made dead,
> While ignoring the lack of a site.

> **The Paperweight Hero**  
> He turned the whole queue to a brick,
> A bureaucratic, immovable trick.
> The data was stuck,
> Just a terrible cluck,
> And he gave it an innocent kick.

> **Haunted Whitespace**  
> The whitespace was suddenly loud,
> With a murmuring, protocol crowd.
> Bricky left them a space,
> In an unreadable face,
> And looked on, formidably proud.

> **Glittering Protocol**  
> His apologies sparkled with dust,
> Like an old filing cabinet rust.
> "Please fill out this slip,"
> With a quiver of lip,
> "And place all your feelings in trust."

> **Footnotes of Passion**  
> His footnotes extended too long,
> Ten levels of bureaucratic song.
> Each subclause divine,
> Each caveat fine,
> While the main text just moaned all night long.
