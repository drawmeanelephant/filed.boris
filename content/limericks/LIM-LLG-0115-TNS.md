**Description:** Ten hand-written limericks about too many feelings for the dashboard, HTTP moods, and the polite reduction of emotional overflow.  
**Updated At:** 2026-07-10

> **Too Many Feelings**  
> The registry ran out of room.
> Too many shades darkened the bloom.
> So sixteen stayed blessed,
> And all of the rest
> Were asked to stand closer for zoom.

> **Two Colors Max**  
> If your feeling took more than two hues,
> The dashboard grew nervous with clues.
> So nuance was stacked
> Till the graph could act
> As if strain came in simpler blues.

> **Do Not Comfort the Form**  
> Some filers grew tender and warm,
> And started consoling the form.
> The addendum replied,
> "Please do not provide
> Reverse care to the survey in storm."

> **HTTP Liaison**  
> When people said, "I feel 422,"
> The Registry wondered what to do.
> So Teapotta appeared
> To decide what was weird
> But still chartable enough to construe.

> **404 Affect**  
> Some feelings got lost on the way,
> No stable path held them in play.
> The system wrote back,
> "We misplaced the track,"
> And filed the whole ache as decay.

> **Dependency Chain**  
> A feeling about feeling was fine
> Until it made three in a line.
> Then the final small node
> Took a 424 code,
> And dependence became the design.

> **Unsafe for Dashboards**  
> Workers sorted emotions by hand:
> Safe for charts, unsafe for the band,
> And accurate too.
> The Panel withdrew
> At the smell of unsanctioned command.

> **Closer Together**  
> No feeling was truly reduced.
> It was only more tightly induced.
> The graphs needed grace,
> So the states took less space,
> And the language got neatly seduced.

> **Teapot Accepted**  
> The liaison attended with spite:
> "I am not interoperable, right."
> Yet even that stance
> Found a sanctioned expanse
> Once the glossary polished it white.

> **Normalization Limit**  
> You can tidy the words on the page.
> You can sort every feeling by cage.
> But the sweep, at its core,
> Could not make there be fewer,
> Only less room for them to rage.

> **The Bruise Palette**  
> Sixteen sanctioned shades for the skin,
> To cover the mess deep within.
> It’s a palette for pain,
> To make it look sane,
> And keep the reality thin.

> **Rough to Smooth**  
> The Normalizer liked it quite rough,
> Till the glossary called out his bluff.
> It polished the grit
> To a corporate fit,
> And called it "transparent" enough.
