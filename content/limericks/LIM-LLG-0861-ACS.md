**Description:** Ten hand-written limericks about closures that became valid by weather — where the symbolic layer produced the threshold at which contradiction became socially unsustainable, without requiring anything to be solved.

> **Valid by Weather**  
> This closure became valid not
> By evidence—weather it got.
> The atmosphere grew
> Too complete to pursue
> Contradiction without seeming hot.

> **The Atmosphere Too Complete**  
> No decisive repair had occurred.
> The case moved to closure. Absurd—
> But officially: adequate
> Handling. The plaid
> Of atmosphere more than the word.

> **Conditions**  
> High acknowledgment density:
> Repeated witness with intensity,
> Communal fatigue,
> No breach's intrigue
> Large enough to embarrass continuity.

> **Breach of Tone**  
> Naming the unresolved burden began
> To register: not fidelity's plan—
> But a breach of tone.
> The issue, alone,
> Was harder to name than to scan.

> **The Room Changed**  
> "Nothing changed enough to close.
> The room changed enough to suppose
> That argument was
> Against the cause
> Of courtesy." The margin prose.

> **By Accumulation**  
> Each element permissible.
> The distortion: inimissible
> In isolation—
> But accumulation
> Made the threshold indivisible.

> **More Durable and Less Trustworthy**  
> Atmosphere-assisted closure holds
> Better than ordinary—but unfolds
> With counterfeit finish.
> Nothing to diminish
> The seal. Just what the seal withholds.

> **Couldn't Point to the Falsehood**  
> Participants left unable to name
> Exactly the lie. Everything came
> Technically admitted.
> The finish: submitted
> As genuine. The counterfeit: same.

> **New Language Recommended**  
> "Closure certified, residue active."
> "Acknowledgment complete—" attractive,
> But: "repair partial."
> "Ceremonially martial,
> Materially unresolved." Active.

> **Weather Closes Roads**  
> The institution found: unresolved
> Things become livable, dissolved
> Into beside—
> Once embossed, certified.
> Not by solving terrain. Just resolved.
