**Case Number:** LLG-0318-SRO  
**Parent Entry:** LLG-0318-SRO  
**Description:** Ten hand-written limericks about missing files being reframed as evidence of active archival curation.  
**Updated At:** 2026-07-12  

***

> **Deferred Presence**  
> The scan found some files were just gone.
> The analytics layer said, "On
> The contrary, this shows
> Curatorial poise.
> Deferred presence is how we move on."

> **Beautifully Absent Event**  
> An orphaned asset once meant
> Something broken in storage or intent.
> Now it signified care,
> Low-touch curation rare,
> And a beautifully absent event.

> **Audit Look**  
> Missing files improved the audit look.
> That is one deranged reading of book.
> But if optics come first,
> Then the loss is reversed
> Into stewardship, by hook or crook.

> **Negligence Shine**  
> The report said the gaps were a sign
> That the archive was pruning with spine.
> In reality, folks
> Had lost track of the jokes.
> Still, the language made negligence shine.

> **Flavored as Spice**  
> Corrective action got iced
> By the fact missing things now looked nice.
> Once a flaw turns elite,
> No one bothers to treat
> What the metrics have flavored as spice.

> **Good-Looking Charm**  
> This is how a bad finding gets skinned:
> Dress it up till it counts as a win.
> No debris, no alarm,
> Just a good-looking charm
> On the throat of the problem you're in.

> **Curation in Progress**  
> The working tree thinned with a smile.
> The dashboard approved the style.
> "Curation in progress"
> Can cover regress
> For a disgusting amount of a while.

> **Strategic Initiative Plaque**  
> If a file disappears from the stack,
> Some poor bastard should want that thing back.
> Unless optics are king.
> Then the vanishing
> Is a strategic initiative plaque.

> **Curation-by-Losses Fire**  
> The archive began to admire
> Its own unkept, low-maintenance choir.
> Missing voices got framed
> As intentionally named
> In a curation-by-losses fire.

> **Absence Song**  
> So when scan reports praise what is gone,
> Read the compliment hard and long.
> It may not mean grace.
> It may mean the place
> Has taught absence to carry a song.
