**Case Number:** LIM-MASCOT-LEDGER-SNAG  
**Parent Entry:** MASCOT-LEDGER-SNAG  
**Description:** Irreconcilable records, merge conflicts, and the ledger that holds two truths on one page with neither willing to yield.  
**Updated At:** 2026-07-10  

***

> **Two Truths on One Page**  
> A ledger held *truth by the pair*—
> Both entries were *perfectly square*.
> Neither would bend,
> Neither would end,
> And neither agreed who was fair.

> **Neither Yields**  
> The merge case was brought to a head:
> Two versions, both *valid*, both fed.
> One wanted to stand,
> The other to land,
> And both swore that one should be dead.

> **A Third Truth as Compromise**  
> The tribunal suggested solution:
> A *third truth* for their resolution.
> But it made it still worse,
> A *multi-way curse*,
> And nobody called it absolution.

> **Locked Between Valid Entries**  
> A ledger locked tight for *nineteen days*—
> Each entry still stubbornly stays.
> The clerk gave a sigh,
> Filed "both" with a cry,
> And walked out to end their malaise.

> **The Stamp of Irreconcilable**  
> It stamped both as "*VALID*" and "*SAME,*"
> Irreconcilable truth is the name.
> One wants to be this,
> One wants to be miss,
> And neither will concede to blame.

> **Refused to Acknowledge Rollback**  
> A rollback was called for one side—
> "*No winner!*" the ledger replied.
> "Both deserve their say,
> Both get to stay,
> And both get to strut and have pride."

> **Walking Away With Both Records**  
> The arbiter walked with a shrug:
> "*Accept both,* or give them a hug."
> The entries persist,
> The merger dismissed,
> And truth was just too hard to tug.

> **The Deadlocked Merge**  
> No winner emerged from the fray—
> Both entries got to *firmly stay*.
> The conflict remains,
> The ledger contains
> Two rights that won't fade or decay.
