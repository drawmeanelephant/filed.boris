**Description:** Four hand-written limericks about one ceremonial ribbon becoming three separate originals because local memory preferred continuity over fabric facts.  
**Updated At:** 2026-07-10

> **Three Originals**  
> One ribbon was somehow the first
> At three different events, unrehearsed.
> Each archive said, "Mine."
> The photos looked fine.
> So fabric got outvoted by thirst.

> **Aggressively Laminated**  
> The dispute stayed polite for two weeks,
> Then got mean in municipal squeaks.
> Every claimant had proof,
> Yet exclusivity's spoof
> When the town wants one object to speak.

> **Save the Good Ribbon**  
> One note said, "Save the good ribbon," and fled.
> Which explained approximately dread.
> Not whose opening day,
> Not whose plaque got the sway,
> Just that everyone worshipped one thread.

> **Shared Certainty**  
> So it rides in a padded valise,
> Under joint seal and rotating peace.
> If you force one true past,
> You lose three towns at last,
> So they archive shared longing, not peace.

> **Satin Triplicity**  
> The ribbon is one, but is three,
> A textile-based holy decree.
> It cut the new bank,
> The bench, and the tank,
> And bends all the laws of degree.

> **Shared Lamination**  
> We laminated it with care,
> To settle the three-way despair.
> Now nobody knows
> Where the heritage goes,
> But at least it’s not breathing the air.

> **Rotating Custody**  
> The artifact schedule is set
> To cover the historical debt.
> If claimants agree
> On a rotational fee,
> The ledger can easily forget.

> **Padded Valise**  
> They put the damn thing in a case,
> So nobody loses their face.
> We rotate the key,
> For municipal glee,
> And hide the procedural waste.
