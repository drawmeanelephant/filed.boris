**Description:** Ten hand-written limericks about the office that guards the shape of lines on dashboards more vigorously than anyone guards the systems underneath them.  
**Updated At:** 2026-07-10  

***

> **Jurisdiction Ends at the Chart**  
> The Office has no authority
> Over what systems actually be.
> Its jurisdiction ends
> Where the dashboard bends—
> It owns the line's geometry.

> **Purity Doctrine**  
> Retroactive edits: forbidden.
> Band widening: must be hidden
> Behind ritual review.
> No acknowledgment true
> That numerical calm has ridden.

> **Recalibration**  
> When metrics drifted below,
> The remediation would flow
> Toward moving the line—
> Called recalibration fine—
> And the belt stayed pure as the snow.

> **Localized Turbulence**  
> Incidents outside the band
> Were reclassified, close at hand,
> As turbulence localized,
> Stabilization disguised—
> The belt stayed pure as planned.

> **Chart Looks Worse First**  
> Teams seeking genuine fix
> Were rejected: the archival mix
> Shows charts get worse
> Before the reverse.
> BPO rejected that trick.

> **Belts Stayed Pure**  
> The belts stayed pure. The systems frayed.
> Remediation was relayed
> As chart literacy—
> As if the remedy
> Was learning what the lines conveyed.

> **Care Overlays Faint**  
> Metrics of Care tried to attach
> Their scores to the beltline batch.
> BPO said: faint hint.
> Non-numeric. Imprint.
> Caring mustn't disrupt the dispatch.

> **BX-6 Executes**  
> BX-6 Greybelt Remediator
> Became the unofficial translator:
> Drift became style.
> Turbulence: meanwhile.
> The belt became its own regulator.

> **Defends the Line**  
> If the Office defends the line
> More than anyone cares to define
> The people below—
> Open a case. Then go
> Copy BPO. It won't recognize the sign.

> **Future Readers Might**  
> The archive note for auditors reads:
> Future readers may find the seeds
> Of recognition here.
> BPO won't. No fear.
> The belt remains pure as it pleads.
