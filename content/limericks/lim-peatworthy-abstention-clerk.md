**Description:** Three hand-written limericks about the Peatworthy Abstention Clerk, counting the dead as present for quorum.

> **Counting the Absent**  
> The member has left us for good,
> But we’re counting him here as we should.
> The Clerk marks him down,
> With a terrible frown,
> And keeps the quorum in the wood.

> **Remembered Casserole**  
> She brought us a dish in the fall,
> So we count her right here in the hall.
> The ghost is assigned,
> To the corporate grind,
> And nobody questions the call.

> **Memorial Quorum**  
> The bodies are absent and cold,
> But the ledger is beautifully bold.
> We vote in the name
> Of the people who came,
> And do what we’re officially told.



> **Buried File**  
> The clerk was abstaining from thought
> The server was totally caught
> We buried the file
> Underneath a big pile
> Of peat that was recently bought

> **Log Jail**  
> A peatworthy process is slow
> It has absolutely nowhere to go
> The human will fail
> And wait in the jail
> Of logs that will constantly grow

> **No Cause**  
> We abstain from making a choice
> We lack a procedural voice
> The clerk is asleep
> The error is deep
> We have absolutely no cause to rejoice

> **Sink in Peat**  
> The bog is procedural dread
> The manual never was read
> We sink in the peat
> With nothing to eat
> And wait for the system to shed

> **Howl at Moon**  
> The clerk left the office at noon
> The server will terminate soon
> The issue is closed
> The data exposed
> We howl at the digital moon
