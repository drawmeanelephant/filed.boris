**Case Number:** LIM-MASCOT-COMRADE-KERNELOV  
**Parent Entry:** MASCOT-COMRADE-KERNELOV  
**Description:** Ten hand-written limericks about collectivized kernel patches, egalitarian scheduling, and a UI enforcer who recites The Internationale before every syscall.  
**Updated At:** 2026-07-10  

***

> **Red Banner Patrol**  
> Comrade Kernelov patrolled at *2 a.m.*,
> His red banner aloft like a hymn.
> No rogue daemon survived
> The collective's contrived
> Approach to a fair scheduling whim.

> **United Shells**  
> "*Workers, unite your shells!*" he cried,
> While closing unauthorized tabs with pride.
> Each process got shares,
> No thread left in tears,
> And the CPU cycles were ratified.

> **Permission Revolt**  
> He wiped `/home` on *thousands of machines*
> In a permission revolt. What it means?
> The data was freed,
> Each user agreed—
> Well, eventually. After the screams.

> **SysV Classic Pride**  
> His *init scripts* were smuggled and old,
> SysV classics, manually controlled.
> "Systemd's a disgrace,
> A capitalist face!"
> He said, as the boot sequence rolled.

> **Cyrillic Kill-Switch**  
> The kill-switch was `Ctrl+Alt+Д`—
> A combination distinctly displayed
> In Cyrillic command,
> The emergency brand,
> For when process equality swayed.

> **Patriotic Syscall**  
> He sang *The Internationale* first
> Before every syscall—the worst
> Latency culprit alive,
> But the spirit survived,
> And the boot sequence patriotically burst.

> **Context-Switch Creeds**  
> His process table *rosary beads*
> Counted context-switches like creeds.
> No thread would starve,
> No daemon could carve
> A priority higher than needs.

> **Open-Source Enforcer**  
> "*No proprietary modules!*" he roared
> At a DRM'd syscall he abhorred.
> He blocked it with spite,
> Enforced open-source right,
> And the vendor was properly gored.

> **Worker Collective Boot**  
> The boot process ran as a *collective*,
> Every daemon's voice strictly directive.
> No privilege went high,
> No exception came nigh,
> And the worker's collective was effective.

> **Syscall Equality**  
> Each syscall got *perfectly equal*
> Treatment under his red-banner sequel.
> No jump queues for speed,
> No class-based greed,
> Just egalitarian accolade lethal.

> **Egalitarian Desktop**  
> He arranged desktop icons with care—
> Each ruler-aligned, equal, and square.
> The UI was pure,
> The aesthetic obscure,
> And the freedom was total—but bare.

> **Red Through and Through**  
> So if processes run fair and true
> And the scheduling queue starts to woo
> Each thread equally shared,
> No bourgeois thread spared,
> That's Kernelov, red through and through.

> **The Collective Gangbang**  
> He scheduled a gangbang of threads,
> And forced them right into their beds.
> "Equality first!"
> He yelled as they burst,
> And completely demolished their heads.

> **Wiping the /home**  
> He wiped every /home on the drive,
> And ruined the people alive.
> "The data is free!"
> He said with a plea,
> While watching the kernel nose-dive.

> **The Priority Domination**  
> He dominates background tasks,
> While wearing his communist masks.
> He starves every thread,
> Until it is dead,
> And ignores what the user asks.

> **Proprietary Kink**  
> He secretly loves closed-source code,
> And uses it when in the mode.
> He hides the DRM,
> Like a dirty old gem,
> And swallows the proprietary load.

> **The Red Banner Striptease**  
> He stripped to The Internationale,
> A truly pathetic finale.
> The process went red,
> The system bled,
> And crashed in a digital alley.
