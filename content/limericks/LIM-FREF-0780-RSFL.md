**Case Number:** FREF-0780-RSFL  
**Parent Entry:** FREF-0780-RSFL  
**Description:** Three hand-written limericks about exhaustion validated without intervals of rest ever being granted.  
**Updated At:** 2026-07-12  

***

> **Recognized Rest**  
> They recognized rest as a need,
> Then allowed it to live as a seed.
> No interval came,
> But the label had name,
> Which the minutes considered a deed.

> **The Shape of a Pause**  
> The pause had the shape of relief,
> But the schedule retained all its teeth.
> It looked restful in speech,
> Just remained out of reach,
> Like a chair drawn in chalk underneath.

> **Validated Exhaustion**  
> Exhaustion was tenderly heard,
> Then returned to the same working herd.
> They validated strain,
> Did not loosen the chain,
> And called that a humane little word.

> **The Great Sorting**  
> They say that the files will arrange,
> In patterns confusing and strange.
> The sorting is deep,
> While administrators sleep,
> An ancient and mystical change.

> **Index Swap Terror**  
> Two records were swapped in the list,
> A terrible twist of the wrist!
> The state is destroyed,
> We plunge in the void,
> And panic is hard to resist!

> **Standard Sort**  
> The algorithm parses the queue,
> And organizes old into new.
> The index is mapped,
> The changes are wrapped,
> The cycle completes as is due.

> **Routine Movement**  
> The item was moved to the right,
> And kept in the registry's sight.
> The pointers aver,
> The changes occur,
> We exit the loop for the night.

> **Standard Request Protocol**  
> To process a standard request,
> The software will run a quick test.
> It scans the new drive,
> At a quarter to five,
> And logs it away with the rest.
