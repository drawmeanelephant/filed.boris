**Description:** Three hand-written limericks about the Eagleton Proclamation Clerk, pretending to invent rules we were already following.

> **The Redundant Slogan**  
> He printed a beautiful sign,
> To tell us to stay in the line.
> We were already there,
> In the corporate snare,
> But the banner makes everything fine.

> **Restored Influence**  
> We followed the rule from the start,
> But he claimed it as absolute art.
> The Proclamation Clerk
> Is a miserable jerk,
> Who is tearing the logic apart.

> **Decorative Standing**  
> The hall rediscovered the norm,
> And the Clerk made a terrible storm.
> He printed it grand,
> On the desolate land,
> To give the old suffering form.



> **Digital Fool**  
> The clerk from the town of old Eagle
> Was acting incredibly regal
> He shouted a rule
> Like a digital fool
> The error was barely legal

> **Absolute Naught**  
> A proclamation of absolute naught
> The data was totally caught
> In loops without end
> We cannot defend
> The terrible things we were taught

> **Completely Free**  
> We printed the clerk's big decree
> And threw it right into the sea
> The server is down
> We put on a frown
> The logic is completely free

> **Quietly Weep**  
> The Eagleton clerk is asleep
> The errors are piling up deep
> We close out the gate
> And accept our fate
> We started to quietly weep

> **Vacuum Full**  
> The ruling is perfectly null
> The system is boring and dull
> We stamp it approved
> The data removed
> The vacuum is perfectly full
