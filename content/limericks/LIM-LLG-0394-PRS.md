**Description:** Four hand-written limericks about an old parade map turning holy enough to overrule safety, permits, and the new pharmacy.  
**Updated At:** 2026-07-10

> **Proper Procession Line**  
> An old map from ninety-eight rose
> From a drawer full of folded old prose.
> Once they called it "the proper,"
> Permits mattered much softer,
> And detours were treated like foes.

> **Spiritually Irregular**  
> The detour round roadwork looked wise,
> Till tradition got tears in its eyes.
> "That turn is profane.
> We've not blessed that new lane."
> Said adults in reflective supplies.

> **Banner Dignity**  
> They argued curb cuts were too thin
> For the banners' symbolic chagrin.
> Which is noble as hell,
> Till the ambulance yells,
> "Move your sacred-ass float line back in."

> **Too Practical to Photograph**  
> The case stayed deliciously stuck:
> Old routes simply looked more like luck.
> No one wants to be named
> As the year it got tamed,
> And the parade started giving a fuck.

> **Sacrosanct Pothole**  
> The detour was functionally sound,
> But heritage fiercely frowned.
> "That pothole is blessed,"
> The chairman confessed,
> And deployed the local bloodhound.

> **Geographic Filing Error**  
> The pavement was formally filed
> Under "Traditions of the Wild."
> When the ambulance came,
> We sorted its name
> And let it be properly exiled.

> **Asphalt Divination**  
> The map from the nineties became
> A scroll with a holy-like name.
> The crossing guard wept
> While the detour line slept
> And the asphalt accepted the blame.

> **Permit Displacement**  
> The logical route was ignored,
> As old paper maps were adored.
> The binder was clear,
> The traffic was near,
> And the permit was slowly unmoored.
