**Case Number:** LIM-MASCOT-BARRELBODY  
**Parent Entry:** MASCOT-BARRELBODY  
**Description:** Limericks about excess payload, oversized attachments, and a mascot that swells further whenever containment is mentioned.  
**Updated At:** 2026-07-10  

***

> **Too Large For the Door**  
> *Barrelbody* came to the gate—
> Too large to squeeze through or to wait.
> It was barely too wide,
> No room on the side,
> And containment would only *inflate*.

> **Three Mail Servers Fall**  
> A single attachment so *wide*
> Took three mail servers down when it tried.
> The bandwidth was stressed,
> The system distressed,
> And nothing but rubble remained as collateral died.

> **Swelling At Every Containment**  
> When someone mentioned a *limit*,
> Barrelbody swelled to a whimsy.
> "You think I won't grow?
> Just tell me to go—"
> And swell past the system's own gimmicky.

> **The Upload Limit Arms Race**  
> The *limit got raised*, then once more—
> Barrelbody swelled to that score.
> It balanced the math,
> Expanded its path,
> Till limits could not hold what it bore.

> **Compressed Into Larger Form**  
> It tried to *compress* just once more—
> The compressed version blocked the door.
> It was twice the size
> Squeezed down with a sigh,
> And left the mail-server stripped to its core.

> **Packaging Larger Than Content**  
> The *wrapper alone* would not fit,
> The packaging served not a bit.
> The contents so small,
> The wrapping so tall,
> That space was consumed bit by bit.

> **Expansion Cannot Be Denied**  
> Whatever you use to *contain*,
> Barrelbody swells once again.
> You mention a lock,
> A buffer, a block—
> And it bursts through your whole countervein.

> **The Oversized Parcel Never Ends**  
> No server can *hold* what it takes,
> No protocol limit it makes.
> It just keeps on growing,
> Forever outflowing,
> Till the whole infrastructure breaks.
