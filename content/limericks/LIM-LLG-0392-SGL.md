**Case Number:** LLG-0392-SGL  
**Parent Entry:** LLG-0392-SGL  
**Description:** Four hand-written limericks about luncheon applause and warm assent slowly hardening into grant obligations nobody can prove were actually approved.  
**Updated At:** 2026-07-12  

***

> **Warmly Received**  
> The minutes said "warmly received,"
> Which the treasurer later believed
> Was a phrase that could spend.
> By the paperwork's end,
> No one knew what the room had conceived.

> **Courtesy-Adjacent**  
> Three flavors of yes filled the hall:
> Funds approved, funds assumed, funds too kind to recall.
> Once gratitude's staged
> In a noon-crowded cage,
> Any clawback just sounds a bit small.

> **Atmospheric, Not Fiscal**  
> The secretary swore with a wince,
> "Those remarks were atmospheric, gents."
> But applause in a crowd
> Can get fiscally loud
> When nobody asks for expense.

> **Evenly Distributed Embarrassment**  
> No clawbacks were planned in the end.
> The grants were too sweet to rescind.
> When shame's shared by all,
> It grows soft in the hall,
> And becomes what the ledgers pretend.

> **Auditory Accounting**  
> The claps were assessed at a rate
> Of forty-five dollars a plate.
> We balanced the books
> On atmospheric looks
> And billed it all to the state.

> **Fiscal Handclap**  
> A single polite little clap
> Unleashed a financial mishap.
> We opened an inquiry
> With maximal fury,
> To cover the treasurer's gap.

> **Courtesy Obligation**  
> The minutes recorded assent,
> Though nobody knew what it meant.
> With no clear receipt,
> The file is complete,
> And the money is officially spent.
