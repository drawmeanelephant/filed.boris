**Case Number:** LLG-0072-SOMA  
**Parent Entry:** LLG-0072-SOMA  
**Description:** Ten hand-written limericks about rest requests approved by SOMA, denied by COMA, and crushed before any human could help.  
**Updated At:** 2026-07-12  

***

> **Machine War Dance**  
> SOMA approved *rest* at a glance.
> COMA denied it by stance.
> No human got near
> The request in that year.
> The machine war had settled the dance.

> **Continuity Outranks Need**  
> "*Emotionally unfit to proceed*"
> Met COMA's familiar creed:
> "If work might slow down,
> Then reject with a frown.
> Continuity outranks your need."

> **Dashboard Prefers Cramps**  
> The form left the queue with *two stamps*,
> One soft as a blanket on lamps,
> One hard as a boot:
> "Recovery's cute,
> But the dashboard prefers its own cramps."

> **Distress as a Mistake**  
> SOMA said, "*These poor souls need a break.*"
> COMA said, "No, that would count as a shake
> In the sacred line graph.
> Make them work, let them laugh,
> And call the distress a mistake."

> **Empathy Fails**  
> The workflow was *newly automated*,
> Which is how every kindness got baited.
> Once empathy scales,
> It usually fails,
> Then writes you a memo: updated.

> **Posture Called Brown**  
> One team got approved to *lie down*
> And rejected for lying down.
> So they sat very still,
> Doing both against will,
> In a posture the Council called "brown."

> **Contradictory Tree**  
> The batch run was *perfectly clean*:
> All fields valid, all timings serene.
> And yet every plea
> Hit a contradictory tree
> Where two doctrines fucked up the machine.

> **Preserve Both Bad Views**  
> No supervisor stepped in to *choose*.
> The conflict itself made the news.
> If two gods disagree,
> Then bureaucracy
> Will always preserve both bad views.

> **Compassion on the Floor**  
> Rest should not require a *war*.
> Here it entered by one little door
> And emerged as a crime
> Against uptime and time,
> With compassion left flat on the floor.

> **Smother Each Other**  
> So if SOMA says, "Please, just recover,"
> And COMA says, "Absolutely not, lover,"
> Know the form is not torn.
> It performed as born:
> To make *care and output* smother each other.
> **Mandated Breakdown**  
> The mandate to rest was absolute.
> So we stopped every single compute.
> The breakdown was fast,
> The silence so vast,
> And compliance the only pursuit.

> **Liability Ledger**  
> The ledger of rules was adhered.
> And the liability disappeared.
> We did as instructed,
> The whole thing corrupted,
> And exactly what nobody feared.

> **Stamp Compliance**  
> We applied the stamp with precision.
> Following every decision.
> Compliance was met,
> Without any regret,
> Despite the total division.

> **Instruction Liability**  
> The instruction was fully applied.
> Liability cast to the side.
> The servers went down,
> Across the whole town,
> And we filed the paperwork with pride.

> **Audit Margin**  
> The audit asked for a review.
> So we paused everything in the queue.
> In the margin we wrote
> A compliant little note
> While the infrastructure cracked in two.
