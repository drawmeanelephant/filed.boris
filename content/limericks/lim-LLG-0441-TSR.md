**Description:** A faded badge, having lost all its core proof, is retained rather than removed to avoid earning a frown under inherited conditions.  
**Updated At:** 2026-07-10  

***

> **The Faded Badge**  
> A surface we trusted of yore,
> Had lost all the proof at its core.
> But taking it down,
> Would earn us a frown,
> So we stamped it and left it on tour.

> **Familiarity's Duty**  
> The ledger had nothing to show,
> The basis had crumbled below.
> Yet habit remained,
> And paperwork gained,
> A permanence no one could know.

> **Inherited Proof**  
> A signature drawn in the dust,
> Continued to carry our trust.
> It wasn't quite true,
> But changing the view,
> Would cause an administrative bust.

> **Governing the Unsettled**  
> We thought we should clear out the tray,
> And throw the old records away.
> But changing the sheet,
> Made everyone bleat,
> So the faded old stamp gets to stay.

> **Renamed Persistence**  
> The archive relies on a ghost,
> A form that we honor the most.
> It's easier far,
> To leave things as are,
> Than question the clerk at the post.
