**Case Number:** LLG-0401-GLP  
**Parent Entry:** LLG-0401-GLP  
**Description:** Limericks about a study record that references itself, where verification requires the data used to verify, and the archive becomes self-sealing.  
**Updated At:** 2026-07-12  

***

> **Archive Self-Seals**  
> A study that studied itself,
> On its own independent shelf.
> The sheet cited the log,
> The log made a clog,
> And the raw data verified itself.

> **Initials That Don't Align**  
> The Director's initials were wrong,
> But the signature had them all along.
> The SOP revised four
> Needed those marks on the floor,
> And nobody knows who'd done wrong.

> **Entry Points Don't Exist**  
> No corridor led in or out.
> Each room was its own roundabout.
> You needed the raw
> To check the same raw,
> And the Tribunal left it in doubt.

> **Compliance is Not Completion**  
> The archive said, "It checks out fine.
> Eleven years in recursive line.
> But we won't verify
> Whether it happened, we'll lie,
> And call that a regulatory sign."

> **Form VER.3.7.2 (RECURSIVE)**  
> Kindy ran the emotional test,
> But the form had admitted its mess.
> Both answers were logged:
> Both confused, both agog,
> The result was: "We're uncertain at best."

> **The Handbook Also Frays**  
> The handbook that cited the rules
> Itself had become one of the tools.
> When you checked what was real,
> You found nothing to feel,
> But a chain of cross-referenced fools.

> **Eleven Years Compliant**  
> For eleven long years it stayed,
> In recursive loop, never swayed.
> The archive accepts
> Compliance neglects
> That the study itself never played.

> **Off-Hand Initials**  
> Bricky signed with his non-dominant hand,
> The initials were crooked, unplanned.
> He filed the deviation log
> While standing in fog,
> And nobody questioned the stand.

> **The Tribunal's Refusal**  
> The Tribunal was asked to declare
> If the study had ever happened there.
> They said, "It persists,
> In compliant lists,
> But we won't vouch for truth—that's not fair."

> **Verification Recursion**  
> To verify, you'd need the proof,
> But the proof's in the same old roof.
> Each source points back
> To its own empty track,
> And the circle resists breaking through.
