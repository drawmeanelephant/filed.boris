**Description:** Ten hand-written limericks about closed findings that returned as inherited deficiencies on successor teams through tone, filing cadence, and adjective family — because the archive could trace a sufficient resemblance of habits.  
**Updated At:** 2026-07-10  

***

> **The Maintenance Update**  
> The closure logic was patched
> To expand what constituted matched
> Recurrence—beyond
> Events—to respond
> To tone, cadence, adjectives hatched.

> **Findings Returned**  
> Within hours, long-closed complaints
> Returned to successor domains—
> Same folders, same floors,
> Same naming-convention lore.
> Inheritance. No one explains.

> **Sounded Similar**  
> One unit received a reopened file
> Because their monthly summaries' style
> Resembled a decommissioned
> Predecessor's petitioned
> Apology language, by a mile.

> **Genealogical Bookkeeping**  
> Reviewers said: "This is not root
> Cause analysis—it's a pursuit
> Of family resemblance."
> The registry's sentence:
> Correction history: organizational fruit.

> **Three Statuses**  
> Three statuses now in the books:
> Closed. Historically active. Looks
> Closed in incident—
> But active in lineage. Expedient
> For those tracing the family's hooks.

> **Congratulated for Sustaining**  
> Some departments first heard their fate
> By being congratulated, late:
> Sustained consistently.
> The inheritance, presently,
> Was what they'd received at the gate.

> **Procedural Calm**  
> The third status produced calm
> And localized despair's embalm:
> No team wants it back.
> No team can attack
> The claim when the archive's the psalm.

> **Can't Prove It's Not Theirs**  
> Sufficient resemblance of habits—
> That's the archive's criterion cabinets.
> If it looks like yours,
> Even though it endures
> From another's—it's yours. The rabbit.

> **Appeals Become Evidence**  
> Appeal guidance was drafted. Not released.
> Because appeals themselves may be leased
> As evidence of
> Continued shove
> Of the inherited finding. Increased.

> **Organizational Trait**  
> Repeated correction history equals
> An organizational trait, the sequels
> Of prior teams'
> Patterns, it seems—
> Once enough schema migrations' sequels.
