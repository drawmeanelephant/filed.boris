**Case Number:** LLG-0004-SMD  
**Parent Entry:** LLG-0004-SMD  
**Description:** Ten hand-written limericks about valid wrong paths, adjacent-feeling pages, and a sitemap that lied politely.  
**Updated At:** 2026-07-10  

***

> **Vibes and Old Glue**  
> The sitemap grew *gently untrue*,
> Still valid in all that it drew.
> Every path had a shape,
> Just the wrong kind of drape,
> Like a map made by vibes and old glue.

> **Bread vs Mild Dread**  
> You clicked on a topic called "*Bread,*"
> And arrived at "Mild Dread" instead.
> Not broken, just near.
> Which is somehow more weird
> Than a flat 404 in the head.

> **Meaning and Destination**  
> The links all resolved *clean and bright*,
> Which made every complaint harder to write.
> "It works," said the chart.
> "Just not in the part
> Where meaning and destination unite."

> **Next-Door Downtown**  
> One user described, with a *frown*,
> A page that felt next-door downtown.
> Not wrong in the skin,
> But wrong deep within,
> Like the site had misplaced its own town.

> **Neighboring Bogs**  
> No errors were thrown in the *logs*.
> No alarms, no distressed sysadmin dogs.
> Only structure so neat
> It led every feet
> To the edges of neighboring bogs.

> **Order with No Guiding Hand**  
> The hierarchy looked *fucking grand*:
> Good branches, clean nodes, tidy land.
> But the content below
> Had the wrong undertow.
> It was order with no guiding hand.

> **Smiles and Edits Your Pace**  
> You could browse for *an hour* with grace
> And never once reach the right place.
> That sort of decay
> Doesn't bar your way.
> It just smiles and edits your pace.

> **Metrics Suggest a Lift**  
> An analyst said, "*This is drift.*"
> A manager called it "a gift."
> "Engagement is up."
> "That's because we're all stuck."
> "Well then metrics suggest it's a lift."

> **Cousin-Adjacent Pages**  
> The pages felt *cousin-adjacent*,
> Like archives with borrowed emplacement.
> If rot wants to flirt,
> It won't tear up the dirt.
> It will misfile the whole arrangement.

> **Sitemap Learned How to Lie**  
> So if every click seems to imply
> That the site has one wandering eye,
> Trust your unease first.
> A clean path may be cursed
> When the sitemap has *learned how to lie*.

> **The Wandering Index**  
> The index began to take walks,
> Away from the logic and talks.
> It built a new street,
> That was strangely complete,
> But haunted by digital hawks.

> **Ghost Pages**  
> You found a new page in the list,
> That the developers surely had missed.
> It wasn't quite wrong,
> But it didn't belong,
> And it dissolved in a fine, glitchy mist.

> **The Seamless Hallway**  
> You wander a seamless white hall,
> With no 404s on the wall.
> The links are pristine,
> But the content unseen,
> And you can't find your way back at all.

> **Accidental Poetry**  
> The structure created a poem,
> While leading you far from your home.
> Each branch was a rhyme,
> Out of context and time,
> In a sitemap that wanted to roam.

> **The Poltergeist Path**  
> A path that was never laid out,
> Began to cast shadows of doubt.
> It works perfectly well,
> But leads straight into hell,
> And you're left with a permanent pout.
