**Description:** Three hand-written limericks about Corridor Heat, the raging, unminuted arguments that actually decide the company’s fate.  
**Updated At:** 2026-07-10  

***

> **The Unminuted Rage**  
> They screamed in the hall till they sweat,
> A meeting they’ll never forget.
> But the packet is clean,
> With a peaceful routine,
> And nobody mentions the threat.

> **Hallway Afterglow**  
> The temperature soared in the space,
> As they cursed at each other's damn face.
> But back in the room,
> They suppress all the doom,
> And govern the project with grace.

> **The Real Decisions**  
> The board doesn’t make the real call,
> It happens right there in the hall.
> The Heat is extreme,
> Behind the regime,
> While the minutes just paper the wall.



> **Melted Floor**  
> The heat in the hallway was bad
> It made all the servers quite sad
> We opened a door
> And melted the floor
> The error was all that we had

> **Painted Red**  
> A corridor painted in red
> The temperature went to the head
> We turned up the fan
> And ruined the plan
> The logic is perfectly dead

> **System Quit**  
> The server is sweating a bit
> The manual doesn't quite fit
> We stand in the flame
> Without any aim
> And wait for the system to quit

> **Procedural Defeat**  
> We measure the corridor heat
> And find it incredibly sweet
> The error is loud
> We step in the cloud
> And accept our procedural defeat

> **Somebody's Cat**  
> The heat is incredibly flat
> We leave the whole database at
> A state of decay
> For many a day
> And blame it on somebody's cat
