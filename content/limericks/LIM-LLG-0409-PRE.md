**Case Number:** LLG-0409-PRE  
**Parent Entry:** LLG-0409-PRE  
**Description:** Ten hand-written limericks about practicing kindness on synthetic people until empathy becomes a reusable script.  
**Updated At:** 2026-07-10  

***

> **Practice Kindness**  
> They built an engine to rehearse
> How directives might soften the curse.
> Not for live use, they said,
> Just to script what gets read
> When someday they have time for the worse.

> **Future Interface Copy**  
> The safest way, strangely, to care
> Was to practice on people not there.
> So synthetic small staff
> Took the burden by half,
> While designers drew comfort from air.

> **Comfort Without Commitment**  
> The library stored by design
> Soft denials in elegant line:
> We see all your strain.
> No, rest will not reign.
> Thank you kindly for holding the spine.

> **Copied Anyway**  
> Though told not to paste into life,
> Some apology scripts met the strife.
> A beta tool shipped
> With rehearsal still clipped,
> And staff felt the synthetic knife.

> **Acknowledge and Move On**  
> PRE knows the official duet:
> Acknowledge, appreciate, yet
> Do nothing that shifts
> Any practical gifts,
> And call that the kindest form set.

> **Style, Not Constraint**  
> The system learned empathy's style,
> Not the rule that would change things awhile.
> So the voice became warm,
> While the old policy form
> Stayed exactly as cold all the while.

> **Compassion Coverage**  
> C.U.N.T.I.E.R. saw in the tray
> A metricable kindness display.
> Compassion coverage soon
> Would inflate by tune,
> While the material help stayed away.

> **Thresholds Waiting**  
> If people start quoting the text,
> Or policy lifts it up next,
> The sandboxed small phrase
> Crosses SSP's gaze,
> And doctrine inherits the flex.

> **Synthetic Voices, Real Templates**  
> What PRE really fashions with care
> Is a shelf of responses to wear.
> When the next hard week hits,
> Someone reaches for scripts,
> And finds yesterday's sympathy there.

> **Said Without Flinching**  
> The archive leaves one cautionary thread:
> If a future interface gently said,
> "We value your quiet,"
> Ask who had to try it
> On people invented instead.
