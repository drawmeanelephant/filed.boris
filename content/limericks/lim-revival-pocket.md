**Case Number:** LIM-MASCOT-REVIVAL-POCKET  
**Parent Entry:** MASCOT-REVIVAL-POCKET  
**Description:** Three hand-written limericks about the Revival Pocket, a tiny, useless zone of hope in a dead system.  
**Updated At:** 2026-07-10  

***

> **The Tiny Zone**  
> One server decided to ping,
> And suddenly everyone sings.
> The Pocket is small,
> In a massive-ass wall,
> Of totally broken-down things.

> **Localized Belief**  
> The Revival is just in your head,
> Because all of the network is dead.
> You stare at the dot,
> And you give it a shot,
> But it’s just a mirage that you fed.

> **Temporary Recovery**  
> It lasted a minute or two,
> Before it exploded anew.
> The Pocket was great,
> But it couldn’t translate,
> To fixing the terrible stew.

> **The False Start**  
> The system booted right up,
> And filled our emotional cup.
> Then it crashed to the ground,
> With a sickening sound,
> Like a terribly beaten-up pup.

> **A Blip of Hope**  
> The Pocket was warm for a sec,
> On the deck of this horrible wreck.
> Then the cold rushed back in,
> With a cynical grin,
> And snapped our collective damn neck.

> **Cruel Optimism**  
> We thought we had finally won,
> And the horrible outage was done.
> But the Pocket just lied,
> And the databases died,
> Before we had barely begun.

> **Uptime Fluctuation**  
> A pocket of uptime appeared,
> Exactly as management feared.
> The server awoke,
> Then immediately broke,
> And standard procedure adhered.

> **Local Optimism Incident**  
> A fraction of hope was deployed,
> Which made the committee annoyed.
> The local reboot,
> Produced a dispute,
> And formally threatened the void.

> **Recovery Acknowledged**  
> The system came back on its own,
> In a highly restricted small zone.
> We logged it as fixed,
> The statuses mixed,
> And left it to wither alone.
