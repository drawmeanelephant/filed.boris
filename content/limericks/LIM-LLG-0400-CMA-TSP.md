> **The Pattern Observed**  
> Staff could name Soft Green Sealie right.
> Staff could not name the case behind
> LC-04's uncertain light.
> The mascot aligned.
> The source file: out of sight.

> **Not Malicious**  
> The drift was not treated as bad.
> It was structurally dangerous, sad—
> A distinction the Council
> Made clearly: the fondle
> Of mascots as doctrine is mad.

> **Indexing vs Substitution**  
> Mascots were once filing aids:
> Memory hooks for unstable cascades.
> That held as long as
> The mascot was a clause
> Of the file, not its accolade.

> Poster captions cited as defined.
> Derivative art without lorelog signed.
> Friendly objects as
> Authoritative jazz.
> Mascot vibes—doctrine resigned.

> **Sealie vs LC-04**  
> Soft Green Sealie: reassurance.
> LC-04: explicit low-clearance,
> Semantically unstable.
> The mismatch: a label
> That settled what remained in variance.

> **Bricky's Margin**  
> "We made a tidy little friend
> And let the friend stand at the end
> For the mess."
> Staff trusted: yes,
> The sticker had a face. Defend.

> **Rounded Corners**  
> Anything with rounded corners now
> Enjoys unearned interpretive vow.
> Authority granted
> By aesthetics—chanted
> Into policy somehow.

> **Not Lying**  
> The mascot is not lying.
> It is merely being applied by
> Belief too efficiently.
> Trusted sufficiently
> Without the source file underlying.

> **Provisional Rules**  
> Every teaching specimen must
> Cite one source case. It's a must.
> Low-confidence icons
> Can't become beacons
> Without an over-coherence trust.

> From authority. Or delayed
> Intake form. Or—third—
