**Case Number:** FREF-0870-WTSL  
**Parent Entry:** FREF-0870-WTSL  
**Description:** Three hand-written limericks about stamping documents, sealing records, and legitimizing conditions that are completely broken.  
**Updated At:** 2026-07-12  

***

> **Seen and Stamped**  
> A witness observed with a seal,
> Making fragile events appear real.
> The mark gave them place,
> Not a remedy's face,
> But a record that others could feel.

> **Reciprocal Witness**  
> They witnessed each other in turn,
> So the file had a steadier burn.
> Not proof it was solved,
> But that someone involved
> Had seen what the system might spurn.

> **Assurance Mark**  
> The assurance mark settled the page,
> Like a dignified clerk on a stage.
> It legitimized pain
> Without breaking the chain,
> Which is seal work at minimum wage.

> **Wait State**  
> The system is waiting to load,
> As dictated deep in the code.
> It waits for a while,
> With corporate style,
> And nobody travels the road.

> **The Eternal Pause**  
> The wait state has frozen the sun,
> A pause that has barely begun.
> The legends foretell,
> A time it runs well,
> But nobody living has won.

> **Lag Spike Doom**  
> A millisecond of delay,
> Has ruined our entire day!
> The company falls,
> Collapse in the halls,
> We're washing the servers away!

> **Idle Loop**  
> The process is paused in the queue,
> Until the next signal is due.
> The CPU sleeps,
> And perfectly keeps,
> The thread in a holding pattern too.

> **No Change**  
> The loop iterated again,
> Returning a value of ten.
> It did nothing more,
> Than wait by the door,
> And logged it with digital pen.

> **Critical Micro-Abrasion Event**  
> The staple was bent by a hair,
> We launched a full-scale questionnaire.
> A breach of the bin,
> A structural sin,
> The desk is now under repair.
