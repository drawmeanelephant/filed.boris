> **Just Minutes at First**  
> A council met, properly briefed,
> For agenda and tone, it was believed.
> Then Bin 8C's shelf
> Took the minutes itself,
> And the schedule came back re-perceived.

> **Adjacency Weight**  
> Set next to the drift logs by chance,
> The minutes acquired a new stance.
> Not description, but mass,
> As if shelving could pass
> For doctrine through interpretive trance.

> **Presence at Cluster Level**  
> Say "cluster-level presence" enough,
> And the graph starts to credit the stuff.
> Peppy, not seen,
> Still attended in green,
> Because lexical density's tough.

> **Retroactive Attendance**  
> No witness confirmed Peppy's chair.
> The registry still placed him there.
> For routing, at least,
> Inferred presence increased,
> And retroactive morale filled the air.

> **Sufficient for Morale**  
> One casual closing line stuck:
> "Materially unclear, but good luck."
> Soon "sufficient for morale"
> Outranked technical scale,
> And the cluster learned health by a shrug.

> **Program Log and Member**  
> The same document now wears two hats:
> Program log and cluster by stats.
> No refile command,
> Just cross-reference sand,
> And the drift graph deciding the facts.

> **Boundary Gone Local**  
> The containment line once held apart
> Council business from doctrine by art.
> Bin 8C replied,
> "Those are neighbors," and sighed,
> Till the border dissolved in the chart.

> **RCI Does Not Care**  
> An operator wrote, dry as chalk:
> "RCI ignores header talk."
> Schedule or creed,
> If the adjacency feeds,
> Custody drifts where the phrases walk.

> **Human-Facing Risk**  
> Treat minutes as harmless too long,
> And the cluster will borrow their song.
> Then tone becomes trace,
> And summary space
> Starts behaving like doctrine gone strong.

> **Contamination by Shelf**  
> So if Council notes leave acting wise,
> Do not blame only authors or eyes.
> In Bin 8C lore,
> Proximity's more
> Than a location; it's how things revise.
