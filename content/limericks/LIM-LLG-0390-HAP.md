**Case Number:** LLG-0390-HAP  
**Parent Entry:** LLG-0390-HAP  
**Description:** Four hand-written limericks about a lodge counting the absent with the dead and the present until nobody knew what quorum meant anymore.  
**Updated At:** 2026-07-10  

***

> **Read Between the Dead**  
> At election, the secretary read
> The absent right in with the dead.
> The room murmured, "Hell,
> That is tender as well,
> But can tenderness chair us?" it said.

> **Spiritually Present**  
> They were morally still in the hall,
> Though they had not been seen there at all.
> When the ballots came near,
> Folks got suddenly clear:
> "Can a ghost make the quorum?" and all.

> **Cruelty by Spreadsheet**  
> No one wanted the minutes to show
> Who first told the estranged they could go.
> So they padded the roll
> With a spiritual poll,
> Which is warm till you need a hard "no."

> **Dignified Visibility**  
> When a merger made bodies run thin,
> Honorary absences wandered right in.
> It's compassionate math:
> Keep esteem on the path
> Till the vote asks whose ass fills a chair.

> **Honorary Vote Threshold**  
> The absent were tallied in full,
> Increasing the quorum's own pull.
> The officer seat
> Was declared incomplete
> By a metric impressively null.

> **Roll Call Bypass**  
> They weren't in the room for the vote.
> The clerk didn't care what he wrote.
> He counted them all
> In the empty old hall,
> And that is the end of the note.

> **Empty Seat Contingency**  
> A missing attendee is dire,
> A threat to the structural fire!
> Their spiritual mass
> Is required to pass,
> Lest the whole institution expire.

> **The Ghostly Electorate**  
> The names from the old vacant chairs
> Have taken to answering prayers.
> They sit in the hall
> Through the winter and fall,
> And manage the chapter's affairs.
