**Case Number:** LLG-0820-MCR  
**Parent Entry:** LLG-0820-MCR  
**Description:** Ten hand-written limericks about perfect care dashboards and real strain fleeing into places nobody measured.  
**Updated At:** 2026-07-10  

***

> **Care Escaped**  
> They scheduled supportive routine
> So no one went seven days unseen.
> On the dashboard: near-perfect.
> In the hallways: still wrecked.
> Care escaped every measured machine.

> **Untouched by the Frame**  
> Serotonin Sam built a pane
> Where the rituals glowed nice and sane.
> The unresolved hurt
> Shifted off the alert
> Into channels untouched by the frame.

> **Carry the Sting**  
> Coverage is not the same thing
> As relief, though the charts love to sing.
> If each check-in is bare,
> Then the metric says care
> While the people still carry the sting.

> **Count of the Tries**  
> Substitution failure's the art
> Of replacing the whole human part
> With the count of the tries.
> Then everybody lies
> That the number itself has a heart.

> **Nested Under the Bed**  
> No unit went unpinged, they said.
> Meanwhile strain nested under the bed.
> It moved out of sight,
> Out of pane, out of light,
> Where the scheduler couldn't make bread.

> **Harder Emotions Move**  
> Automated rituals can soothe
> In a limited, modesty groove.
> But if they're the proof
> Of all care under roof,
> Then the harder emotions all move.

> **Smart Prey**  
> The dashboard achieved near-total sheen.
> That phrase should make anybody mean.
> When care becomes score,
> People route pain elsewhere
> Like smart prey avoiding a screen.

> **Allegedly Reclaim**  
> Metrics of Care is a name
> That should not be allowed without shame
> If the metric displaces
> The needs and the faces
> It was allegedly built to reclaim.

> **Hid Somewhere Else**  
> Ritual is not relief by itself.
> Neither is a clean graph on a shelf.
> This system learned fast
> How to look unsurpassed
> While real strain went and hid somewhere else.

> **Fee for Elegance**  
> So if care coverage looks airtight,
> Check the rooms just outside of the light.
> What the dashboard can't see
> Often carries the fee
> For the elegance shown in the site.

> **One Click Survives**  
> The single "I feel acknowledged" stays.
> The complex support interaction decays.
> Data minimisation
> Loves simple emotion—
> The harder ones vanish from gaze.

> **Archival Bias**  
> What the record remembers of care
> Is what fit in one click, one square.
> The rest is inferred
> From statistics heard
> About what was probably there.

> **Thin as a Sheet**  
> The care was as thin as a sheet,
> In a way that was nearly a treat.
> It massaged the "void,"
> Until all were employed,
> In a dashboard that's neatly complete.
