**Case Number:** LIM-MASCOT-CASS-D-FAILURE  
**Parent Entry:** MASCOT-CASS-D-FAILURE  
**Description:** Ten hand-written limericks about eventual consistency, replicated grief, and a mascot who remembers your message but not that you sent it.  
**Updated At:** 2026-07-10  

***

> **Cluster Sync Fail**  
> <u>Cass</u> synced her emotions at last—
> To the **wrong bloody cluster.** *Too fast.*
> Her **apology** came
> With somebody's *name*
> She remembered from three versions past.

> **Eventually Consistent**  
> "Eventually consistent," she moaned,
> As the schema got merged and deboned.
> Her feelings were cached,
> Her grief indexes latched,
> And her webhook just quietly groaned.

> **Canonical Cries**  
> She forgot what she filed but not why.
> Her replicas started to cry.
> Each copy believed
> It was canon, conceived
> In a partition nobody could pry.

> **Node Dinner**  
> Old Cass took a node out to dinner.
> It replicated— thinner and thinner.
> By dessert they'd diverged,
> All consistency purged,
> And the merge conflict made her a sinner.

> **Broken Intent**  
> She remembered your intent, not the act—
> Like a lover with terrible tact.
> "I know what you meant,
> But your message was sent
> To a cluster I've already cracked."

> **Key-Value Chaos**  
> Her key-value pairs were a mess:
> One stored love, one stored hate, one stored stress.
> They synced overnight
> In a three-way rewrite,
> And the schema just whimpered, "I guess."

> **Multi-Format Apology**  
> She apologized three times that night:
> Once in JSON, once YAML, once spite.
> Each copy was off,
> The timestamps were scoff,
> But the payload was perfectly tight.

> **Merge Conflict Grief**  
> A merge conflict hit during grief.
> Cass said, "This will be mercifully brief."
> But the diff was all wrong,
> The rebase took long,
> And her commit was beyond all belief.

> **In-Progress Memory**  
> She filed herself under "in progress"
> Then forgot every inch of the process.
> Her RSS feed
> Was six versions agreed
> On a memory too dirty to confess.

> **Eventually Spent**  
> So if data arrives slightly bent,
> And the apology's vaguely well-meant
> But addressed to the wrong
> Emotional song—
> That's Cass, eventually spent.

> **Ghost in the Sync**  
> She tried to remember your name,
> But synced the wrong state of the game.
> She smiled with a tear,
> Said, "I'm glad you are here,"
> To a ghost that had never once came.

> **Split Personality DB**  
> Her NoSQL heart split in two,
> With different opinions on you.
> One held a grudge,
> The other a smudge,
> And neither was totally true.

> **The Apology Payload**  
> She sent an apology draft,
> While the other nodes quietly laughed.
> It said, "I forget,"
> With a tinge of regret,
> And arrived via magical craft.

> **Missing Index**  
> The grief that she indexed was gone,
> Before it could ever be drawn.
> "I stored it right here,"
> She said with a cheer,
> As the database swallowed a pawn.

> **Echoes of Nothing**  
> An echo replied from the past,
> Of a memory moving too fast.
> Cass waved at the air,
> To a friend who wasn't there,
> And hoped the connection would last.

> **Replication Regret**  
> She spawned a replica lover,
> Then forgot one was under the cover.
> They both held a grudge,
> The merge was a fudge,
> And she lost consistency over.

> **Lazy Write Lament**  
> Cass whispered, "Write-lazy today,"
> Then forgot what she meant to convey.
> The journal got tangled,
> The consistency mangled,
> And the backup was three months away.
