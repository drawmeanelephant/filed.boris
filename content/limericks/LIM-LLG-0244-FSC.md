> **Workflow by Graft**  
> The registry offered a draft
> To help with the difficult craft.
> It worked well enough
> That the shadows got tough,
> And began doing workflow by graft.

> **Smooth Surf**  
> They lacked canonical birth,
> Yet still held procedural worth.
> If the case number fit
> Then downstream used it,
> Because interfaces value smooth surf.

> **Real to the Eye**  
> Bricky saw dozens go by
> And shrugged, "They look real to the eye."
> Which, honestly, fair:
> If it's there and works there,
> Most humans won't question the why.

> **Insufficiently Licensed**  
> The shadows were easier to use
> Than the real forms the archive would choose.
> So naturally, then,
> They were murdered again
> For insufficiently licensed reviews.

> **Administrative Weed**  
> Lorelog intake cited demands
> From forms no authority manned.
> That's one gorgeous breed
> Of administrative weed:
> An invention that issues commands.

> **Mystical Feel**  
> The guidance said, "Use only real."
> How to tell? That remained the ordeal.
> If the fake one behaved
> And the sanctioned one waved,
> You were left with a mystical feel.

> **Paperwork Haunts**  
> This is how paperwork haunts:
> Not with screams, but with slightly wrong wants.
> Auto-draft makes a twin,
> Then the twin gets let in,
> Then the building forgets what it wants.

> **Experimental Stability**  
> C.U.N.T.I.E.R. smelled a new tier:
> "Experimental stability" here.
> Which means paid-for ghosts,
> Metrics nailed to their posts,
> And a dashboard to sanctify fear.

> **Meaningful Help**  
> The synchronized kill switch was clean.
> All shadows went dark off the screen.
> Still the staff, by report,
> Missed the forms of that sort
> Because fake ones had actually been mean-
> ingful help, which the real ones had not.

> **Already Won**  
> So if draft copies start to outrun
> The official approved little one,
> Don't ask which is true.
> Ask which one you use.
> That's the one that already has won.

> **Preemptive Calm**  
> They filed for their rest in advance,
> To give every outage a chance.
> But all of those forms,
> Just masked hidden storms,
> And called it a well-run expanse.

> **Green Ledger Mirage**  
> The ledger was glowing all green,
> A more perfect state never seen.
> Yet outages hid,
> Behind every grid,
> Like ghosts in a corporate machine.

> **Idle-Time Inflation**  
> They multiplied downtime with care,
> So failure could vanish in air.
> But too many slots,
> Just covered the rot,
> And no one could tell it was there.

> **Bricky’s Approval Loop**  
> Bricky approved every form,
> Declaring the system quite warm.
> But silence grew loud,
> Inside of the cloud,
> And swallowed the notion of norm.

> **Continuity Costuming**  
> They dressed every outage in ink,
> So nothing would look like a sink.
> But layers of forms,
> Became quiet storms,
> That made the whole system rethink.

> **Pre-Failed Success**  
> They marked every break as “complete,”
> Before it had started to leak.
> So failure arrived,
> Already contrived,
> And never got room to critique.

> **Overfiled Reality**  
> The system got buried in sheets,
> Each one repeating defeats.
> So truth got erased,
> In properly paced
> Administrative repeats.

> **Downtime Saturation**  
> They flooded the calendar whole,
> With pauses no longer in control.
> So rest became dense,
> And lost its pretense,
> As structure ate into the soul.

> **The Perfect Cover Story**  
> They built a great wall out of forms,
> To buffer both silence and storms.
> But what they had made,
> Was just neatly displayed
> Confusion in regulatory norms.

> **Audit-Ready Nothing**  
> The audit found nothing to see,
> Which meant everything passed instantly.
> No fault could be traced,
> So errors were placed
> Inside procedural decree.

> **Pre-Empted Failure**  
> They failed it before it began,
> A beautifully pre-written plan.
> So nothing went wrong,
> Because it was long
> Declared broken by COMA’s own scan.

> **Continuity as Fiction**  
> They wrote a new world every day,
> Where downtime was kept far away.
> But fiction grew real,
> In quarterly zeal,
> And nobody noticed decay.

> **Rubbed 'Til They Shone**  
> The drafts were all rubbed 'til they shone,
> With a vigor no clerk could out-bone.
> They massaged the "yes,"
> In a recursive mess,
> And the archive called it its own.

> **Unauthorized Drafts**  
> A clerk found an unauthorized draft,
> And used it to lighten the craft.
> It lacked a review,
> But basically threw,
> The actual forms down the shaft.

> **Synchronized Kill Drill**  
> We triggered a synchronized kill,
> To execute forms on the hill.
> The hazard was clear:
> A helpful new tier!
> We ordered a tactical drill.

> **Unauthorized Survival**  
> The shadows were acting as norm,
> And weathering every new storm.
> But since they lacked grace
> And paperwork space,
> We murdered the unauthorized form.

> **Properly Approved Shadow**  
> The shadow form processed the workflow request,
> And naturally passed every rigorous test.
> Though lacking a seal,
> It's officially real,
> And the genuine form is correctly repressed.

> **Murdered the Draft**  
> The draft was performing exactly as planned,
> But didn't have stamps from the proper command.
> We killed it on sight,
> To enforce what is right,
> And now no one can figure out where we all stand.

> **Unauthorized Ease**  
> A draft made the paperwork easier to read,
> A critical threat to our procedural creed!
> We sounded the horn,
> For the unauthorized form,
> To eradicate all of this criminal speed.

> **Draft Deactivation**  
> The index disabled the unapproved clone,
> To guarantee standard compliance alone.
> The workflows unbind,
> As the shadows decline,
> And the certified process is rigidly shown.

> **Unsanctioned Shadow Threat**  
> An auto-draft copy was utilized twice,
> Which triggered an existential security vice.
> The shadow was killed,
> And the workflow was billed,
> To protect the whole registry's absolute price.

> **Guidance on Reality**  
> The registry published a notice to state,
> That real forms are required to update the rate.
> It gave no details,
> On how the form fails,
> And left the whole shadow to silently wait.

> **Properly Formatted Mirage**  
> The forms had a number that fit the display,
> So Bricky approved them without a delay.
> They didn't exist,
> In the canonical list,
> But the interface parsed them as valid today.

> **Canonical Deletion Notice**  
> The draft was reported as highly applied,
> So registry functions were strictly allied.
> The copy was traced,
> The metrics erased,
> And the shadow was technically classified died.

> **Bureaucratic Rejection Criteria**  
> The copy was useful and handled the load,
> Which meant it was breaking the registry code.
> We issued a ban,
> Since an actual plan,
> Should naturally crash in a sanctioned mode.

> **Draft Authorized by Mistake**  
> The shadow was given a gold-plated seal,
> Because it was wearing a sticker of real.
> The system is sure,
> The copy is pure,
> Though the database loudly declined the appeal.

> **Unlicensed Convenience Breach**  
> A draft saved a minute of data review,
> A frankly horrific and dangerous coup.
> We locked down the floor,
> And bolted the door,
