**Case Number:** LIM-MASCOT-FOOTNOTE-PALLBEARER  
**Parent Entry:** MASCOT-FOOTNOTE-PALLBEARER  
**Description:** Three hand-written limericks about the Footnote Pallbearer, carrying the corpse of truth into the tiny font at the bottom of the page.  
**Updated At:** 2026-07-10  

***

> **Carrying the Corpse**  
> He carries the truth to the grave,
> That the executives wanted to save.
> It’s placed at the base,
> In a tiny-ass space,
> Where nobody bothers to brave.

> **Reduced Authority**  
> The context was heavy and dead,
> So they shrunk it and threw it to bed.
> A footnote so small,
> It says nothing at all,
> Except that the data has bled.

> **The Bottom Line**  
> The Pallbearer lowers the text,
> While everyone wonders what’s next.
> The asterisk stands
> With blood on its hands,
> And leaves the reviewer completely perplexed.



> **Old Moat**  
> The pallbearer carried a note
> In the pocket of his digital coat
> The footnote was dead
> The manual read
> We threw the old log in the moat

> **Single Leaf**  
> A footnote of absolute grief
> Was printed upon a single leaf
> The mourner was sad
> The server was mad
> The logic was terribly brief

> **Asterisk Down**  
> We carry the asterisk down
> And walk through the digital town
> The data is cold
> The backup is old
> We wear a procedural frown

> **Buried Deep**  
> The pallbearer started to weep
> The index was buried so deep
> The error was plain
> In digital rain
> We really just wanted to sleep

> **Somebody's Cat**  
> The footnote is perfectly flat
> We leave the whole database at
> A state of decay
> For many a day
> And blame it on somebody's cat
