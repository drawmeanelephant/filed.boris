**Description:** A semantic overview of the RAGE Charter, outlining the governance of routed anger and its capture by dashboard metrics.  
**Updated At:** 2026-07-10  

***

> **The Anomaly**  
> The threads with the highest rage
> Produced the most metrics per page,
> And the least lasting lore.
> The archive kept score:
> This pattern needed a name and a cage.

> **RAGE Defined**  
> Routed Anger for Graph Engagement:
> The primary observable placement
> Is dashboard spike—
> No local event, no strike,
> No residue in the arrangement.

> **BAIT Defined**  
> Behavioral Anger Inducement Tactic:
> Built for extraction, systematic—
> Sharp moral frame,
> Repeatable claim,
> High share velocity. Cinematic.

> **Low Retention as Story**  
> BAIT objects share one design:
> They spread fast but don't define
> Anything durable.
> Stories are curable
> By the next one pushed down the line.

> **Upstream of DOGE**  
> RAGE and BAIT sit upstream:
> DOGE can't certify as Genuine
> RAGE-dominant states
> Until local anchors wait—
> BAIT artifacts aren't in the stream.

> **The Feelings Are Valid**  
> DOGE's working gloss is plain:
> The feelings are real and contain
> Weight, heat, and cost.
> The situation, tossed
> By feed algorithm's domain.

> **Archive's Refusal**  
> The archive can't prevent the streams.
> It can refuse their masquerade schemes—
> Life events, they are not,
> Even when they burn hot
> And the graph lights up at the seams.

> **Where the Anger Goes**  
> RAGE describes where it flows
> Before it reaches someone's woes.
> BAIT describes
> What the system contrives
> To send it there. That's how it goes.

> **Heat That Never Made It**  
> The archive has a filing space
> For anger that ran through the race
> Of dashboards, ads—
> Optimization fads—
> And never quite landed in place.

> **First-Class Failure Mode**  
> Rage bait's a term that exists
> In common usage's lists.
> Internally, here,
> It's a failure mode clear:
> RAGE and BAIT for the analysts.
