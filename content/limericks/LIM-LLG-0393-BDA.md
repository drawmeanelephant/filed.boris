**Description:** Four hand-written limericks about a decorative banner slogan becoming real governance before anyone was brave enough to call it policy.  
**Updated At:** 2026-07-10

> **Heritage Only, Sure**  
> They hung up a banner for show,
> Just heritage, warm little glow.
> By the end of the night
> It was settling each fight,
> Like stitched fabric had learned to say "no."

> **Pride Must, Per Banner**  
> The slogan said pride should be useful.
> Which sounds nice till it starts sounding forceful.
> When parade duty came,
> Folks invoked the dead frame,
> And the room found the needle resourceful.

> **Authority Disputed**  
> No motion had made it a rule,
> No bylaw had christened the tool.
> But quote it enough,
> And committee-folk bluff
> Like embroidery chaired the damn school.

> **Popular Enough to Obey**  
> The trouble with dead people's phrasing
> Is it enters the room pre-amazing.
> Soon the volunteer chart
> Gets assigned by textile art,
> And dissent starts to look like grave-razing.

> **Decorative Legalities**  
> We hoisted the banner for flair,
> To cover a crack in the stair.
> By half-past eleven,
> It governed like heaven,
> And taxed the new folding chair.

> **Unratified Stitches**  
> The textile was hung in the hall,
> With no formal motion at all.
> Yet schedules were made,
> And volunteers paid,
> Based on the words on the wall.

> **Influence Logged**  
> A slogan was found in a bin.
> They placed it right next to the pin.
> The meeting adjourned,
> The ledger returned,
> And nobody questioned the spin.

> **Operational Embroidery**  
> The thread had instructed the room.
> We filed it as "Loom-Given Doom."
> The clerk was concerned,
> The paragraphs burned,
> To manage the fabric's perfume.
