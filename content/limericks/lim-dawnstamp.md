**Case Number:** LIM-MASCOT-DAWNSTAMP  
**Parent Entry:** MASCOT-DAWNSTAMP  
**Description:** Limericks about early execution, replay risk, and the entity that arrives before the room agrees it is ready.  
**Updated At:** 2026-07-10  

***

> **The Sun Has Not Risen**  
> Dawnstamp arrived with the *dawn*—
> Before the system was drawn.
> The batch job ran early,
> Arrived all too fairly,
> When nothing was ready or spawn.

> **Just A Little Too Early**  
> The job was about to begin—
> Then Dawnstamp rushed in from *within*.
> "I'm early," it said,
> "Before you've spread,"
> And chaos erupted of sin.

> **Tomorrow's Date Stamped Today**  
> A document marked was *one day*—
> Before it had time to delay.
> The timestamp was wrong,
> The morning not strong,
> And replay risk entered to stay.

> **Before The Room's Agreement**  
> The room didn't know it was *time*—
> But Dawnstamp had already chimed.
> The process began,
> Before any plan,
> And systems unraveled sublime.

> **Early Execution Tracer**  
> The tracer marked *early* that trace—
> Left on unprepared system's face.
> "You weren't ready yet,"
> Marked Dawnstamp, and bet
> That replay would win the whole race.

> **Connections Not Fully Opened**  
> The connection was *halfway complete*—
> When Dawnstamp sent up the beat.
> "I'm early," cried Dawnstamp,
> "Let's execute—cramp
> This risk until systems retreat."

> **Stamped With Tomorrow's Hour**  
> A timestamp was *stamped ahead*—
> Of when it should happen instead.
> "Come back," the system said,
> "But Dawnstamp's led—
> I've already come," it replied fed.

> **Early Risk of Replay**  
> The risk of a *replay* is high—
> When Dawnstamp's arrived way too nigh.
> The batch job won't wait,
> The hour strikes late,
> And processes fail with a sigh.
