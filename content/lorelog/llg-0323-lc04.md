## Object Summary

LC-04 Soft Green Seal is a graphical mark applied to certain reports and dashboards.  
It is most often described in internal guidance as:

- “the continuity reassurance indicator,”
- “the quiet interval health mark,” or
- “the seal that means this part is fine.” 

No directive has published a formal definition.  
The seal’s presence is treated as self-explanatory; its absence is seldom documented.

## Origins in Assurance Vocabulary

During the terminology revisions recorded in LLG-0323-ASD, the Assurance Vocabulary Desk sought a visual alternative to words such as failure and unresolved. 

The Soft Green Seal was introduced as a generic emblem to accompany phrases like:

- favorable persistence condition,
- deferred assurance state,
- emerging structure under observation. 

AV-14 Nullseal Register lists LC-04 among the first optics attached to rephrased findings, but does not specify what underlying condition each seal instance was meant to represent. 

## Procedural Uses

LC-04 is known to appear in at least three contexts:

- **Audit Summaries**  
  Placed next to sections where recurring issues have been linguistically harmonized but not structurally resolved.

- **Continuity Dashboards**  
  Displayed over time blocks with no recorded incidents and no declared downtime, regardless of reported strain.

- **Metrics of Care Overlays**  
  Used to mark intervals where some acknowledgment of care was logged, even if no change in schedule or load occurred. 

In each case, attaching the seal is considered a completion step; removing it is not a defined operation.

## Directive Readings

- **SOMA** tends to read LC-04 as “care was at least noticed here,” especially when it appears alongside narrative notes.  
- **COMA** reads it as “continuity requirements were satisfied,” sometimes treating the seal as equivalent to passing a threshold.  
- **C.U.N.T.I.E.R.** treats the seal as a convenient segment boundary when graphing healthy-looking intervals. 

Because these readings are never reconciled, the same mark can later be cited as proof that:

- rest was allowed,
- uptime was unbroken,
- care was measured. 

## Low-Confidence Status

LC-04 qualifies as a Low-Confidence Object because:

- it is heavily referenced in guidance (“ensure a green seal is present for all healthy intervals”),
- its absence is not logged as an error,
- its semantic content cannot be reconstructed from surviving documentation. 

Some teams apply LC-04 purely by template: if the report renders, the seal prints.  
Others apply it manually based on local impressions of “good enough.”

Lorelog cases note that neither practice is wrong, because there is nothing to measure them against. 

## Interaction with Metrics of Care

LLG-0820-MCR reports that early metrics-of-care experiments tried to treat LC-04 as an anchor:

- intervals with LC-04 and positive care notes were graphed as “attended stability,”
- intervals with LC-04 but negative or absent care notes were labeled “silent endorsement,”
- intervals lacking LC-04 were ignored unless an incident forced attention. 

This mapping failed to stabilize, partly because the seal was easier to add than the care was to perform.  
Charts began to show more green than anyone recognized from experience.

The experiment was quietly retired; LC-04 remained in the UI. 

## Examples and Fragments

Recovered snippets mentioning LC-04 include:

- “Apply Soft Green Seal where continuity optics would benefit from reassurance.”  
- “Do not withhold the seal solely due to unfiled discomfort; escalate feelings separately via SOMA.”  
- “If removing LC-04 would require a new directive, leave it in place and open a Lorelog case instead.” 

No snippet specifies a condition under which LC-04 must be removed.

## Archival Guidance

Catalogers are advised:

> When reviewing historical dashboards, assume LC-04 meant
> “nobody successfully argued otherwise at the time.”  

LC-04 is therefore preserved as:

- an assurance artifact,
- a continuity gesture,
- an unresolved statement that “this part is fine enough, until someone insists on looking closer.” 

No deprecation has been proposed; the Classification Stability Panel regards its ambiguity as structurally helpful.
