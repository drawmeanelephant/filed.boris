***



DOGE exists because the archive eventually needed a way to tell the
difference between experiences that *happened to someone* and experiences
that *happened to their feed*.

At first, this distinction was considered unnecessary.
The systems assumed that if something was filed with enough intensity
and enough supporting metrics, it could be treated as a real event.
That assumption failed quietly, then loudly, as more and more filings
described days that consisted entirely of scrolling.

DOGE is the department that was created when nobody could say with
confidence which parts of the lorelog still described lives.

---

## Jurisdictional scope

DOGE holds provenance jurisdiction over any experience that is:

- submitted for canonical archival as a life event,
- cited as emotional evidence in cross-directive disputes, or
- routed into breedingProgram-adjacent registries as proof of care.

DOGE does **not** decide whether:

- the experience was meaningful,
- the filer was correct to feel what they felt, or
- the world should have been different.

DOGE evaluates only:

- **Origin** — where the experience first made contact.
- **Agency** — how much the filer could change, interrupt, or leave it.
- **Residue** — what remained after the refresh interval ended.

Experiences that fail DOGE’s criteria are not deleted.
They are reclassified into simulator categories and kept under
different evidentiary rules.

---

## The three canonical tests

DOGE applies three tests, in order.
All may pass, some may pass, or none may pass.
The result determines classification, not belief.

### Test 1 — Origin

Did the experience arise from the filer’s local world
(body, place, relationships), or did it arrive pre‑assembled through
a feed, notification, dashboard, or recommendation surface?

Origin is assigned to the earliest point the filer can name.
If the only identifiable origin is “the algorithm showed it to me,”
Origin receives reduced weight and the experience becomes a candidate
for simulator classes.

### Test 2 — Agency

During the experience, did the filer have a way to alter its
character, duration, or outcome?

- Leaving the room, ending the call, or refusing the task all qualify.
- Reacting only through platform affordances (likes, replies, boosts)
  is recorded, but carries lower agency weight.
- Being unable to mute, block, or meaningfully withdraw counts
  against agency, not against the filer.

### Test 3 — Residue

Does anything of the experience persist once the immediate
consumption window has closed?

DOGE treats residue as structural change:
shifts in behavior, reference, or decision-making,
not just vivid recall.

A strong emotion that leaves no residue beyond the next refresh
interval is eligible for reclassification as high-intensity
feed artifact. The hurt is real; the provenance is not certifiable.

## Relationship to breedingProgram (LLG-0375–0378)

DOGE maintains an adjacency channel to the breedingProgram refuge
cluster for experiences involving synthetic-origin entities.

For those cases, Origin is not used to disqualify.
The department’s stance is:

> A genuine experience of care does not require the object of care
> to have originated locally. It requires the care itself to have.

Care that arises locally, even toward non-local entities,
is eligible for full DOGE certification, subject to the other tests.

---

## Archive position

DOGE is not a correctness engine.
It is a modest bureaucracy for refusing to let graphs,
rage streams, or corporate Greek be the final word
on what a life contained.

Its only power is annotation.
For the archive, that has always been enough to matter.

### DOGE — Department of Genuine Experiences, Charter and Jurisdictional Scope

The Department of Genuine Experiences holds absolute jurisdiction over the authenticity of your sighs.

The charter dictates that an experience is only genuine if the department has successfully taxed it.

Our mandate is to ensure that no one suffers or rejoices without first obtaining the correct permits.

If you feel something outside of DOGE jurisdiction, you are technically engaging in emotional smuggling.

The Department of Genuine Experiences was founded on the principle that reality is too dangerous to be left unadministered.
