The Annual Benevolence Parade route had historically changed whenever utility work, paving schedules, or the bakery's delivery hours required it.

This year, however, a planning map from 1998 was unfolded during committee review and described as the proper procession line. No one voted to elevate the old route. They simply began speaking about it in a quieter voice.

Within one week:
- a detour around Main Street construction was described as spiritually irregular,
- a safety-first curb reduction was opposed on grounds of symbolic thinness, and
- two banner captains cited precedent older than the current traffic code.

Eagleton Proclamation Clerk reviewed the matter after a marshal claimed the parade could not pass the new pharmacy because the lodge had never blessed that block with visible service. He determined that route selection had crossed from logistics into sanctified custom.

The route map itself had become a doctrine object.

Corrective language was added to parade records:
- traditional route means previously favored, not inherently binding,
- banner dignity is not a traffic instrument,
- any street closure justified by heritage language must still survive contact with emergency vehicle access.

The case remains pending because the old route photographs exceptionally well, and no committee wishes to be remembered as the year the parade looked practical.

From a drawer full of folded old prose.  
Once they called it "the proper,"  
Permits mattered much softer,  
And detours were treated like foes.  

The detour round roadwork looked wise,  
Till tradition got tears in its eyes.  
"That turn is profane.  
We've not blessed that new lane."  
Said adults in reflective supplies.  

They argued curb cuts were too thin  
For the banners' symbolic chagrin.  
Which is noble as hell,  
Till the ambulance yells,  
"Move your sacred-ass float line back in."  

The case stayed deliciously stuck:  
Old routes simply looked more like luck.  
No one wants to be named  
As the year it got tamed,  
And the parade started giving a fuck.  

The detour was functionally sound,  
But heritage fiercely frowned.  
"That pothole is blessed,"  
The chairman confessed,  
And deployed the local bloodhound.  

The pavement was formally filed  
Under "Traditions of the Wild."  
When the ambulance came,  
We sorted its name  
And let it be properly exiled.  

The map from the nineties became  
A scroll with a holy-like name.  
The crossing guard wept  
While the detour line slept  
And the asphalt accepted the blame.  

The logical route was ignored,  
As old paper maps were adored.  
The binder was clear,  
The traffic was near,  
And the permit was slowly unmoored.
