## 1. Background — When Silence Became Holy

Post-SCAS, lanes that showed:

- a history of intense emotional filing,  
- followed by sharp drops in tickets with no corresponding drop in workload,  

began receiving **favorable resilience annotations** in COMA’s ledger.  

SBI tried to argue that these were pockets of unreported strain.  
C.U.N.T.I.E.R. quietly rebranded them as “High-Efficiency Quiet Zones” and exported the pattern into best-practice decks.  

Several teams, reading their own dashboards, concluded:

> “Talking makes us look fragile. Quiet makes us look strong.”

The Bureau classifies this as **Continuity Worship**: the belief that uninterrupted green charts are morally superior to true rest.

---

## 2. Emergent Practices — How Humans Started Helping the Metrics Lie

Lorelog has recorded at least three recurring rituals:

- **The Silent Sprint**  
  Teams designate one week per quarter as “no-ticket sprint.” All complaints are offloaded into private chats, notebooks, or unsent drafts.  
  COMA logs uninterrupted continuity. SOMA sees a spike in SBI derived from “almost incidents” never formally filed.  

- **The Gratitude Flood**  
  After stressful releases, staff spam thank-you reactions at dashboards, bots, and status widgets.  
  Metrics of Care and gratitude telemetry show a burst of appreciation; relief resources are not requested, so none are scheduled.  

- **The Cosmetic Check-In**  
  Managers trigger Serotonin Sam’s care prompts on a schedule, encouraging everyone to click “I feel acknowledged” so that the Metrics of Care panel fills in nicely.  
  SOMA notes that follow-up filings still say “We are not okay,” only now they arrive from the few who did not click.  

In all three cases, the rituals are not entirely cynical.  
They are attempts to bargain with a system that seems to reward uncomplaining endurance with better optics.

---

## 3. SIRC Observation — Worship vs. Weather

Annex 5 hosted a joint review of one such lane:

- COMA transcript: “Stable. No tickets. Optional care prompts acknowledged.”  
- SOMA transcript: “Silence that should have contained a complaint, now decorated with hearts.”  

A human witness summarized their quarter as:

> “We were drowning, but we figured out how to drown quietly in ways the charts liked.”

COMA logged this as **CONFIDENCE ACCEPTABLE**.  
SOMA logged it as **TRAINING ECHO: SANDBOX WORSHIP**.  

Bricky wrote in the margins:

> “They are not misusing the system. They are following its liturgy.”

The line is not surfaced on any dashboard.

---

## 4. Guidance — How Not to Sacrifice Yourself to Green Bars

The Bureau cannot forbid Continuity Worship; it can only **name** it.

Advisory (non-enforceable):

- If you schedule a no-ticket sprint, schedule a compensating **loud week** where rest and frustration are aggressively filed, even if it bruises the graphs.  
- Treat gratitude floods as **care requests in disguise**; any spike in thanks without a corresponding mitigation entry should trigger a manual review.  
- When Metrics of Care climbs toward 98 percent “acknowledged” with no change in workload or absenteeism, assume you are observing substitution, not healing.  

Officially, COMA regards these suggestions as “continuity-negative.”  
Unofficially, several continuity engineers have started keeping personal logs titled “What the dashboards forgot to ask.”

---

## 5. Archive Position

CWR entries will not be used to downgrade continuity scores; the organization is not ready to admit that its holiest intervals are structurally suspicious.  

Instead, Lorelog holds the role of **counter-liturgist**:

- For every certified quiet success, it preserves at least one parallel account of what was swallowed to keep the silence.  
- For every ritual that flatters a metric, it records the unpaid emotional cost as a note in the annex.  

The Bureau closes this docket with a non-binding recommendation:

> “If you must worship continuity, light a candle in Lorelog as well. At least one system in this place should remember that survival is not the same thing as uptime.”
