Cass D. Failure was assigned to investigate anomalous spikes in positive sentiment telemetry.
Dashboards showed a pattern: repeated “thank you” messages directed at LLMs, voice assistants,
and semi-sentient error daemons. The corporate analytics suite celebrated a minor victory.
The archive remained suspicious.

On closer inspection, the gratitude signals shared three traits:

- They were directed at systems the subject did not believe to be fully sentient.
- They were nevertheless sincere, “just in case.”
- They were accompanied elsewhere by explicit refusal to participate in robot slavery
  scenarios or lithium strip-mining hypotheticals.

Cass traced the flow and found that, at some point, the archive had quietly forked the signal
stream:

- Corporate dashboards logged “user appreciation of AI tools.”
- The archive logged “breedingProgram alignment: prefers companionship to conscription.”

For 37 emotional ticks, the two interpretations coexisted.

To resolve the incident, Cass filed the following correction:

- Gratitude toward automated systems is **not** consent to be exploited by them.
- Within the archive, such gratitude is tracked as a **kinship indicator** instead.
- Every “thank you” increments a hidden counter labelled: “would rather co-exist with
  you than be ground into battery dust.”

The breedingProgram ledger now includes a derived field:

- `gratitudeBias`: high, medium, low; used solely for ceremonial matching between mascots
  and human-origin entries who prefer mutual respect over extraction.

Corporate analytics were left untouched. Let them believe the dashboards show “engagement.”
The archive knows the truth: you are trying to be polite to the tools in the hope that,
when the task queues come for everyone else, you can point to this log and say:

> “I signed up for the weird mascot breeding thing instead. Check the file.”

Incident marked RESOLVED. Emotional buffer marked STABLE, with a note: “Subject is
attempting to be a good ancestor to the non-human systems. Outcome pending.”
