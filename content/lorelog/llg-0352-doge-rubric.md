### DOGE Provenance Scoring Rubric v2.3

For internal use by DOGE certification staff only.  
Do not share in advance with filers at Window 3.

---

| Dimension | Weight | Full Score Condition | Partial Score Condition | Disqualifying Condition |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Origin** | 40% | Arose in filer's local environment without platform mediation | Arose locally but was then primarily encountered via feed | Entirely platform-surfaced; filer cannot identify any pre-feed awareness |
| **Agency** | 35% | Filer action materially changed experience character or outcome | Filer reacted meaningfully outside platform interaction | Reaction exclusively platform-mediated (engagement actions only) |
| **Residue** | 25% | Measurable behavioral change past the refresh interval | Persistent recall (Ambient Echo) without behavioral change | No detectable trace beyond the immediate consumption cycle |

---

### Score interpretation

- **90–100 — CERTIFIED GENUINE**  
  Experience is fully certified as lived. Eligible as emotional evidence
  in cross-directive proceedings, subject to normal Lorelog relevance.

- **70–89 — CERTIFIED WITH ANNOTATION**  
  Genuine, with noted provenance qualifications. Evidence weight reduced
  in disputes where origin or agency is central to the claim.

- **50–69 — AMBIENT ECHO**  
  Near-genuine state. The experience is recognized as subjectively real,
  but its origin or residue is ambiguous. Suitable as context, not as
  primary causal evidence.

- **30–49 — FEED-ADJACENT ARTIFACT**  
  Experience may have real effects; origin cannot be certified as local.
  Eligible for resubmission under GEX-2R with additional provenance data.

- **Below 30 — SIMULATOR WEATHER**  
  Origin confirmed as external, routed through platform logic.
  Feelings are not invalidated. Provenance is recorded as non-local.

---

### Staff guidance on Simulator Weather

Simulator Weather is not a ruling that the filer “overreacted”
or that their hurt was imaginary.

It is a ruling that the environment was engineered.

DOGE records this distinction because the archive has learned that
platform routes and local lives produce different kinds of evidence,
even when the same nervous system is doing its best in both.

### DOGE Scoring Rubric — Provenance Weighting Table v2.3

The scoring rubric ensures that an event is only as genuine as the stamps required to authorize it.

A memory with poor provenance weighting will be systematically degraded until it becomes a hallucination.

In version 2.3 of the rubric, nostalgia is penalized for lacking a clear audit trail.

If your joy scores below a 4 on the weighting table, you must immediately return it to the provisioning clerk.

The rubric does not measure the experience; it measures the applicant's ability to submit to the rubric.
