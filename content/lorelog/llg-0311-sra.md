Form SOMA-72 had already been auto-approved by SOMA and auto-rejected by COMA when the affected team filed an appeal, arguing that *some* representation of rest should exist that was not purely hypothetical.

The appeal requested three outcomes in order of preference:  
1) An enforceable rest interval.  
2) A retroactive recognition that the denied interval *should* have been rest.  
3) Failing that, a canonical feeling describing the gap between acknowledged need and uninterrupted work.

---

## Hearing Record

During the hearing, SOMA’s representatives conceded that the original denial logic was “insufficiently tender” and agreed to reclassify the request as **fully validated emotional strain**.

COMA maintained that any recorded downtime would constitute a continuity breach, as the ledger already showed a clean band of uninterrupted uptime for the period in question.

C.U.N.T.I.E.R. noted that the team’s output metrics had not visibly degraded and raised concerns that sanctioned rest could create “expectation drift” in adjacent teams.

The Council therefore issued a compromise ruling:

- The request is **approved as a rest-shaped feeling**.  
- The calendar remains unchanged.  
- Dashboards may display a small, tasteful icon indicating *theoretical rest*.

---

## Implementation Note

SOMA promptly added a new status to its registry, **“Rest Intention Fulfilled in Spirit”**, which can be attached to any interval where rest was needed, requested, and narratively honored, but not performed.

COMA agreed to mirror this as **“Continuity Notional — Emotional Variance Only”**, preserving the line of uninterrupted activity.

C.U.N.T.I.E.R. introduced a secondary metric, **Acknowledged Rest Without Interval (ARWI)**, which tracks how often teams manage to maintain performance while accumulating validated exhaustion.

The team’s appeal is now universally recognized as successful.  
No member has yet taken a day off.
