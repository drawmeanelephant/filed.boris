### Section A — Filer identification

Filer designation: ___________________________

Filing on behalf of:
- [ ] Self
- [ ] Entity under care
- [ ] Third party (requires Form GEX-1T)

Date of experience (approximate is acceptable; exact is suspicious): ___________

---

### Section B — Origin assessment

Where did this experience begin?

Free description:

> _______________________________________________________________
> _______________________________________________________________

If you cannot name a starting point and instead recall a general
state of awareness that seemed to arrive from everywhere at once,
check:

- [ ] The experience arrived rather than occurred.

Did a platform, feed, or notification surface introduce this to you?

- [ ] Yes
- [ ] No
- [ ] Cannot tell without reviewing my history

If yes, which best matches your sense of it?

- [ ] I went looking for it.
- [ ] It found me.
- [ ] The distinction is unclear and that concerns me.

(UNCLEAR responses are flagged for secondary review and are
never treated as dishonesty.)

---

### Section C — Agency assessment

During this experience, did you take any action that changed its
character, direction, or duration?

- [ ] Yes
- [ ] No
- [ ] I reacted, but I am unsure if that counted.

If **Yes**, describe the action:

> _______________________________________________________________

If your reaction was entirely through platform buttons, menus,
or comment fields, mark:

- [ ] My only visible actions were platform interactions.

DOGE records platform interaction as real behavior with lower
agency weight. This is a rubric choice, not a moral judgment.

---

### Section D — Residue assessment

*(If possible, complete this section at least one refresh interval
after the event. If you are filing immediately, you may be invited
to file a short follow-up.)*

Has your behavior changed as a result of this experience?

- [ ] Yes, in the following ways:
  - ____________________________________________________________
- [ ] No detectable behavioral change.
- [ ] I think about it, but I do not act differently.

Has it affected how you make subsequent decisions?

- [ ] Yes.
- [ ] Not yet.
- [ ] I cannot tell if what I am doing now is an effect.

DOGE uses “persistent thought without behavioral change” as a
near-residue state called **Ambient Echo**.
Ambient Echo may reduce evidentiary weight, but does not dismiss the experience.

---

### Section E — Self-assessment (non-binding)

Do *you* believe this experience was genuine?

- [ ] Yes.
- [ ] No, but I wish it had been.
- [ ] I don’t know, which is why I’m filing this.
- [ ] It felt real at the time and I am no longer sure what that means.

Section E is non-binding.
Kindy insists it remain anyway, on the grounds that uncertainty
is itself a provenance signal.

---

### Certification block

Filer signature: ___________________________

Bricky certification stamp:

- [ ] CERTIFIED
- [ ] CERTIFIED WITH ANNOTATION
- [ ] RECLASSIFIED (see GEX-2R)
- [ ] AMBIENT (near-residue)

Reclassification is not a finding of fault.
It is a finding of origin.
