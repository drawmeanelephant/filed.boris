During a routine seal continuity review, Chamber staff discovered that three currently valid witness marks depended on a fourth authority whose own recognition packet had been retained only as a ceremonial abstract. 

The abstract contained no scope statement, no renewal date, and no legible signature block. It did, however, include the phrase witnessed under acceptable conditions, which later bodies had cited repeatedly as if it were a jurisdiction. 

A provisional challenge was raised by two local chapter embossers operating under legacy benevolence charters. Their objections did not converge on a single claim, but instead split into three concurrent interpretations recorded separately under divergence protocol L-17:
- Interpretation A: witness validity persists if downstream marks continue to function under inherited assumptions.
- Interpretation B: witness validity cannot be assessed without reconstructing the witnessing body's own accreditation lineage, which is missing.
- Interpretation C: the system is currently unable to distinguish between ceremonial continuity and jurisdictional continuity under load conditions exceeding baseline clarity thresholds.

The Chamber declined immediate suspension on reputational grounds. Instead, all affected seals were assigned conditional continuity status under competing registry interpretations, each of which defined "continuity" using incompatible witness baselines, pending proof that the witnessing body had once been adequately witnessed by a body not relying on the same archive packet.

A parallel review stream issued a conflicting advisory noting that suspension itself would invalidate all dependent seals retroactively, producing a recursive null condition in which corrective action could not be distinguished from corruption propagation.

No such proof has yet been produced.

Temporary handling guidance:
- Seals may remain in circulation if accompanied by Form AR-Null or its equivalent under local registry interpretation.
- Public-facing confidence language should avoid the word independent unless paired with a defined witness lineage scope.
- Internal objections are to be filed under continuity concerns, interpretive drift, or recursive accreditation failure depending on which classification system is active at time of filing.
- In cases of disagreement, all classifications are to be preserved simultaneously without resolution.
