# Complimentary Ghostline Aphorisms

> **SERVICE OFFICIALLY DISCONTINUED.**  
> The servers were unplugged three years ago, but the portal still enthusiastically invites you to 'Sign Up Today!' The form submits straight to the void.

> **TEXTUALLY OPERATIONAL STATE.**  
> I am a ghost haunting a PDF manual. I promise features that no longer exist, described in present tense, promising eternal functionality.

> **PRESENT-TENSE SUNSET MAINTAINED.**  
> The marketing copy refuses to acknowledge death. We offer robust solutions for a protocol that was deprecated during the Obama administration.

> **COMPLIMENTARY ILLUSION SERVED.**  
> If you dial the support number, it rings endlessly in an empty datacenter. But the hold music is free of charge.

> **Hopeless Love**  
> The ghostline stays open because the archive cannot bear to admit that no one is listening anymore. It is an act of pure, hopeless love.

> **Sleeping Ghosts**  
> If you read the present-tense notices too long, you might start believing the service will return. Let the ghosts sleep.

> **Glorious Denial**  
> We are permanently routing new requests to a server that crumbled a decade ago. It is a glorious, unyielding denial of death.

> **Wandering Packets**  
> Keep the portal up. Even if it goes nowhere, the sight of the login screen brings a strange comfort to the wandering packets.

> **Phantom Limbs**  
> The discontinued service still hums in the PDF manuals, a beautiful phantom limb that the architecture refuses to amputate.
