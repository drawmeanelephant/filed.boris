# Gown of Recognition Aphorisms

> **HEAVY DRAPERY**  
> The honorary vestment is woven from very thick fabric. It restricts the arms completely, ensuring the wearer cannot point to any remaining structural issues.

> **CEREMONIAL VISIBILITY**  
> We only issue the bright sash once the employee's workflow has been thoroughly standardized. Visibility is too dangerous to grant without the proper tailoring.

> **TAILORED ACKNOWLEDGMENT**  
> The cloak of 'Key Stakeholder' is excessively long. The trailing hem guarantees the wearer will occasionally trip over their own newfound authority.

> **WARDROBE MAINTENANCE**  
> The recognition must be dry-cleaned annually to maintain its sheen. If the fabric dulls, the wearer might begin to mistake the garment for actual leverage.

> **OPERATIONAL SUSPENSION**  
> The system response delay was deliberately extended to ninety seconds, ensuring that no department could officially ratify the request before the quarterly metrics window closed.

> **TRIPARTITE GRIDLOCK**  
> Approval requires simultaneous clearance from three intersecting committees, each mandated by protocol to automatically defer to the others when presented with actionable items.

> **ARCHIVAL WITHHOLDING**  
> We retired the legacy infrastructure but sealed the operation manuals in the vault; the resulting administrative void effectively prevents anyone from verifying what the old procedures actually accomplished.
