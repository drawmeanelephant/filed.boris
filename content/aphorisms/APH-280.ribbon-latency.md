# Ribbon Latency Aphorisms

> **CEREMONIAL LAG EXPERIENCED.**  
> The software shipped three weeks ago. We are holding the launch party today. The enthusiasm is stale, but the catering is paid for.

> **DELAYED OPENING ENERGY.**  
> I am the oversized scissors arriving after the ribbon has already been torn by frustrated users. I pose for the photo anyway.

> **MOOD DEPARTED. RITUAL CONTINUES.**  
> The crisis is over. The team is exhausted. The executives have just arrived to formally announce the beginning of the effort. We smile.

> **LATENCY DECORATED WITH SCISSORS.**  
> The event and the celebration of the event are separated by an unbridgeable gulf of calendar invites. I live in the gap.

> **CRITICAL DELAY NORMALIZED.**  
> The emergency response protocol took four business days to authorize. The disaster has concluded, but we are prepared to intervene.

> **SCHEDULED PANIC INITIATED.**  
> The structural collapse was scheduled for Tuesday. The notification arrived on Thursday. We will conduct the mandatory panic in a controlled environment next week.

> **POSTPONED ACCOUNTABILITY CEREMONY.**  
> The system collapsed on Tuesday, but the formal inquiry cannot commence until the commemorative plaques are distributed next month. Priority must remain with the scheduled pageantry.

> **INAUGURAL EMERGENCY PROCEDURE.**  
> We are inaugurating the new disaster recovery protocol with a grand opening event. The ongoing structural fires will have to wait for the conclusion of the executive speeches.
