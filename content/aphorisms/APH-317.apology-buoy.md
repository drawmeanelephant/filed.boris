# Apology Buoy Aphorisms

> **FLOATING REMORSE DEPLOYED.**  
> We are deeply sorry for the inconvenience. Please cling to this hollow plastic sphere of regret while the ship of reliability sinks beneath you.

> **STABILIZING STATEMENT ISSUED.**  
> The apology bobs on the surface of the outrage. It does not calm the storm, but it provides a brightly colored marker of where the disaster occurred.

> **CORPORATE SORROW MEASURED.**  
> Our regret is profound, structurally rigid, and hollow inside so it doesn't sink under the weight of our actual legal liabilities.

> **SURFACE NAVIGABILITY MAINTAINED.**  
> Navigate around the apology. Do not attempt to stand on it. It is entirely buoyant and contains zero solid solutions.

> **Anchored Regret**  
> The buoy bobs gently in the wreckage. It fixes nothing, but we love it because it is the only thing left that acknowledges we drowned.

> **Plastic Sorrow**  
> Do not trust the floating apology. It is bright and hollow, designed to distract you while the heavy truths sink out of sight.

> **Sinking Comforts**  
> The corporate sorrow was deployed perfectly. It looked so beautiful floating there that we almost didn't notice the water filling our lungs.

> **Preserved Wrecks**  
> We catalog the apology buoys in the archive. They are the bright, plastic tombstones of every catastrophic mistake we ever learned to forgive.

> **Bobbing In The Dark**  
> Long after the ship has vanished, the statement of regret bobs alone in the dark water, a steadfast and lonely beacon of our failure.
