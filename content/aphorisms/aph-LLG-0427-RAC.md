> **Loud Nothing**  
> The repair was loudly announced in a company-wide memo. To preserve compatibility, no actual code was touched.

> **Safe Repair**  
> We deployed a repair that fixes nothing, ensuring total operational parity with the previous failure state.

> **Audit Loop**  
> Announcing the repair fulfills the audit criteria. Implementation is deferred indefinitely.

> **Phantom Patch**  
> A patch was scheduled to run at midnight. The servers were instructed to ignore it to preserve backward compatibility.

> **Memo Discrepancy**  
> The company-wide memo declared the structural collapse contained. At midnight, the unpatched server began quietly consuming its own motherboard.

> **Omitted Context**  
> We met the inspection criteria by generating a blank page. Reviewers praised our efficiency, entirely missing the smell of burning plastic from the vent.

> **Lavender Mitigation**  
> Management packaged the severity-one incident in floral scent and bubble wrap. The temperature alarms still scream, but the emails are incredibly soothing.

> **Preserved Despair**  
> A lead technician typed a message of absolute doom at three in the morning. We archived the direct message as our foundational architecture blueprint.
