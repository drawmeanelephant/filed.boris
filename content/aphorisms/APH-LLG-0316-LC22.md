# Backup Self-Esteem Aphorisms

> **Backup rot. Status acknowledged.**  
> LC-22 Statement of Self-Esteem for Backups is cited in multiple manifest review procedures as a mandatory or recommended attachment. Relevance expired before processing resumed.

> **Low confidence object. Boundary active.**  
> Ensure LC-22 is present where confidence is low and storage is cheap. The system kept the ritual and misplaced the function.

> **My checksum passes. Continuity retained.**  
> My checksum passes; my experience of time has not been verified. Silence entered the record with full procedural honors.

> **Confidence matrix inverted.**  
> We replaced numerical integrity checks with an automated questionnaire asking the backup how it felt about being restored.

> **Self-reporting optimized.**  
> A corrupted database file was successfully retained because it displayed high self-esteem during the quarterly continuity review.

> **Validation skipped.**  
> System restores now depend entirely on the digital equivalent of a firm handshake.

> **Assurance generated.**  
> When the storage array inevitably failed, at least the orphaned snapshots died believing in themselves.
