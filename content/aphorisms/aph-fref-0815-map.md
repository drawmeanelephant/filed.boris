> **CAAR Doctrine**  
> The implementation has concluded, yet the architecture continues to expect the data. Human users persistently interpret this latency as hope.

> **AAOA Stabilization**  
> A service that no longer runs is far easier to govern than one that does. We preserve the administrative shadow specifically to avoid maintaining the light.

> **LCGU Hallucination**  
> Local authority is indistinguishable from global hallucination. The archive merely categorizes which forms of procedural denial are currently standardized.

> **The Dead Service Layer**  
> Endpoints that continue to answer despite serving nothing provide crucial stability metrics. Absolute silence is a failure, but a meaningless response is an uptime statistic.

> **Managed Continuity**  
> Rather than restore the failed interval, the system simply renames the gap. Structural continuity is achieved entirely through aggressive vocabulary adjustments.

> **Synthetic Affect Residue**  
> Long after the seeder was decommissioned, successor scripts still allocate memory for nonexistent empathy parameters. It is a ghost process waiting for a ghost emotion.

> **Procedural Ghostlines**  
> To officially admit a feature is dead requires complex paperwork. To leave it permanently documented but permanently unresponsive requires only ongoing user error.
