# Staged Sobriety Aphorisms

> **POST-CRISIS COMPOSURE ATTAINED.**  
> The servers have stopped smoking. We straighten our ties and adopt expressions of grave professional concern for the cameras.

> **VISIBLE SERIOUSNESS DEPLOYED.**  
> We were screaming in the hallway ten minutes ago. Now, in the town hall meeting, we speak in slow, measured tones about 'structural learnings.'

> **EMOTIONAL OPTICS REGULATED.**  
> The panic has been carefully folded and placed in a drawer. The face we present to the shareholders is made of calm marble.

> **PREVENTABLE HEAT IGNORED.**  
> We do not mention who left the stove on. We only discuss our profound commitment to fire safety going forward. Look how solemn we are.

> **A Marble Mask**  
> The crisis broke our hearts, but the mask we wear to the shareholders is flawless marble. We polish it daily so they never see the cracks beneath.

> **Folding the Panic**  
> Fold the panic neatly and place it in the drawer. When the town hall meeting is over, we will open it again and let the quiet terror breathe.

> **Grave Expressions**  
> The servers burned to the ground, but our ties were perfectly straight. The optical perfection of our grief almost made the loss worthwhile.

> **Cherished Solemnity**  
> We file the staged sobriety in the permanent record. It is a beautiful, necessary lie we tell to protect the fragile system from its own heat.

> **Structural Silence**  
> The hallway screams are gone. Now we speak in slow, measured tones, cherishing the quiet performance of a recovery that hasn't happened yet.
