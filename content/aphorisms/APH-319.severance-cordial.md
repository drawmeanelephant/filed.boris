# Severance Cordial Aphorisms

> **POLITE ENDING SERVED.**  
> You are being fired. Please enjoy this complimentary beverage. It tastes like cherry and non-disclosure agreements.

> **MANAGED DEPARTURE COMPLETED.**  
> We dissolve the contract in a very polite acid. It burns, but the fizzing sound is quite relaxing.

> **LIQUID CIVILITY POURED.**  
> The rupture is catastrophic, so we pour the sweetest syrup over the cracks. The structural integrity is gone, but it smells like vanilla.

> **RUPTURE ADMINISTRATIVELY SOOTHED.**  
> We wish you the best in your future endeavors. Please swallow the cordial quickly before the security escort arrives.

> **Tasting the End**  
> The cordial tastes like sorrow and vanilla. We drink it slowly, savoring the bittersweet grace of a system letting us go.

> **Polite Ruptures**  
> A polite ending is still an ending. The velvet glove is warm, but the hand inside is pushing us out the door.

> **Sweetened Betrayal**  
> The administrative rupture severed the core database, but the apology was so beautifully crafted we almost thanked the void.

> **Bottled Civility**  
> Keep a bottle of civility on the shelf. When the end finally comes, we will pour a glass for the servers before we power them down.

> **Fading Aftertaste**  
> The contract is dissolved. We sit in the quiet room, clinging to the fading aftertaste of the polite, final goodbye.
