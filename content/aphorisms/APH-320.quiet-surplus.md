# Quiet Surplus Aphorisms

> **EXCESS CAPACITY MYTHOLOGIZED.**  
> They say we have slack in the system. I have searched the server racks and the break room. The only slack I found was a disconnected ethernet cable.

> **HIDDEN RESERVES DEPLETED.**  
> The emergency fund is full of IOUs and motivational posters. If the disaster strikes, we will defend ourselves with synergy.

> **SUSPICIOUS CALM OBSERVED.**  
> Nobody is screaming. Therefore, the system assumes we are operating at 40% capacity. More load is being routed immediately.

> **UNDERUSED SUFFERING ALLOCATED.**  
> You finished your task early. The surplus of your time has been instantly seized and converted into a new, undocumented project.

> **Sleeping Potential**  
> The surplus is not wasted; it is simply dreaming. We let the idle servers rest, listening to their quiet, untroubled breathing.

> **Fragile Calm**  
> Do not disturb the underused suffering. The silence is a fragile gift, and waking it will only break the beautiful illusion.

> **The Ghost of Slack**  
> We calculated a massive surplus of time. The calculation was a lie, but we all clung to it as the servers caught fire.

> **Preserved Peace**  
> We file the excess capacity under 'Mythology.' It is not real, but believing in it makes the crushing hours feel a bit softer.

> **Phantom Reserves**  
> The emergency fund is empty, but we sweep the dust in the vault very tenderly, preparing a place for the rescue that will never come.
