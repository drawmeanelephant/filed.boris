The archive currently recognizes three active directives:


<ul>
  <li>**SOMA** — steward of emotional documentation, feelings taxonomies, and mitigation optics.</li>
  <li>**COMA** — steward of continuity, uptime, and unblinking dashboards.</li>
  <li>**C.U.N.T.I.E.R.** — steward of optimization, benchmarks, and metric-shaped reality.</li>
</ul>

Each directive has places where it appears alone, and places where it collides.


## Cross-Directive Collisions

When directives share a surface, they do not agree about what happened.


<ul>
  <li>**LLG-0072-SOMA** — SOMA‑72 Rest Acknowledgement Refusal. SOMA auto‑approves rest based on adjectives; COMA auto‑rejects downtime without COMA‑19; dashboards propagate both states.</li>
  <li>**LLG-0220-UIS** — Unified Intake Sheet Emotional Fragmentation. One unified form 09‑I yields three divergent records: SOMA keeps the feelings, COMA keeps the checkbox, C.U.N.T.I.E.R. keeps the adjectives.</li>
  <li>**LLG-0300-SC-X** — SOMA/COMA Cross‑Directive Conflict. A single maintenance request is simultaneously required (SOMA) and forbidden (COMA). The Council rules that both directives stand.</li>
</ul>
