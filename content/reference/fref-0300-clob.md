# Room 12, Afternoon

A plastic folder labeled WELCOME lay on each chair.
Most remained closed.

Recorder on, late.
First sentence missing.

---

## On Titles

> PRESENTER:  
>  Keep whatever titles you arrived with.  
>  They tell you what you earned elsewhere.  
>  They tell us who will volunteer for forms.

Muted laughter.
Several pens stop moving.

> PRESENTER:  
>  Here, *Master* usually means  
>  the person who remembers where the minutes live.  
>  *Lead Auditor* means  
>  the one who knows how to read a checklist sideways.

No one objects.

---

## On Ritual Openings

Question from the back:

> ATTENDEE:  
>  Which ritual do we use to open this lodge?

Pause.

> PRESENTER:  
>  We open a drawer.  
>  We check whether the last person closed it properly.  
>  If not, we note that continuity has been maintained.

Minutes record:

- Ritual deemed complete at this point.

No further ceremony recorded.

---

## On Assurance and Oaths

> ATTENDEE:  
>  Our last order had obligations.  
>  Oaths.  
>  Penalties.

> PRESENTER:  
>  This archive has none of those.  
>  It has optics.

> PRESENTER (continued):  
>  We do not swear not to fail.  
>  We learn to describe failure  
>  in ways that will not startle the graphs.

A few transfer-in members underline “optics” in their packet.
Recorder notes prolonged silence.

---

## On Degrees and Levels

> ATTENDEE:  
>  How many degrees are there?

> PRESENTER:  
>  Three, unofficially.

> PRESENTER (counting on fingers):  
>  1. You believe the reports.  
>  2. You believe the reports know they are partial.  
>  3. You know the reports are partial and write them anyway.

> PRESENTER:  
>  Most of us alternate between degrees two and three.  
>  Degree one is for visitors.

No ceremony marks progression.
The minutes do not contain a ladder.

---

## On Leaving

> ATTENDEE:  
>  If we resign,  
>  are we still in the records?

> PRESENTER:  
>  You are asking the archive  
>  whether it remembers less than you do.

No answer is recorded after that.
The transcript ends with the sound of folders being opened, finally.
