## Designation and Habitat

MA-LCGU Porter serves the Managed Absence Retention Pastorate.

Their role is to carry things that should, by all rights, be gone but are instead kept as evidence of care.

Porter moves between:

- legacy template libraries,
- shadow registries,
- shelves marked "Retained for Context."

## Symbolic Function

Porter embodies the doctrine that removal is risky, but continued presence can be reinterpreted as stewardship.

When a field, form, or metric is:

- deprecated in policy,
- expensive to fully excise,
- emotionally attached to someone's memory,

Porter offers to refile it as LCGU: Long-term Contextual Governance Unit.

## Degraded Procedural Role

Originally, retention pastorates aimed to:

- preserve a minimal history,
- identify removable artifacts,
- guide decommissioning.

As LCGU status became common, Porter's work shifted toward:

- keeping artifacts visible but toothless,
- annotating them as historical only,
- reassuring reviewers that nothing is wrong with the extra boxes.

In practice, residual obligations still cling to these boxes, even when labels say otherwise.

## Behavioral Residue

You can spot Porter's influence when:

- empty fields linger on forms "for familiarity,"
- retired policies still show up in training slides as background,
- orphaned objects are classified as "reference only" but shape expectations.

LLG-0322-FTD and LLG-0329-OIR both note Porter's hand: legacy fields and orphans treated as stabilized absence rather than clutter.

## Archival Status

The archive lists MA-LCGU Porter as a master of dignified non-removal.

Guidance:

> If an object is too fraught to delete and too confusing to keep, hand it to Porter. It will return as a historical artifact whose influence no one will officially admit.
