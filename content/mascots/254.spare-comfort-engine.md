Spare Comfort Engine is a reserved artifact for those moments when institutions deploy backup tenderness rather than primary relief.

Birthed during the capacity failure of LLG-0851-CSI, it was spun up by the Auxiliary Care Systems when the main empathy routines exhausted their quota. Its primary function is not to heal, but to certify overflow panic states by issuing unconvincing but technically compliant warmth receipts.

Unlike a Primary Empathy Core, which actually attempts to resolve trauma, the Spare Comfort Engine merely logs that an attempt at comfort was computationally executed—a distinction that leaves a cold, bureaucratic residue.

Its horsepower is unknown.
Its timing is suspicious.
