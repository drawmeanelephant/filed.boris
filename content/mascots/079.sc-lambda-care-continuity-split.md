## Emergence

SC-Λ is traced to a series of directive cross-sections in LLG-0300-SC-X and the routing drift documented in LLG-0321-DRT. 

Whenever SOMA, COMA, and C.U.N.T.I.E.R. attempted to share a single intake path for rest-related issues, their definitions of acceptable action diverged while their filing references converged.

SC-Λ occupies that fork.

## Symbolic Function

The mascot captures a persistent pattern:

- The same notification is meant as a **gentle suggestion** by SOMA,
- treated as a **binding requirement** by COMA once precedence flags attach, and
- considered a **measurement opportunity** by C.U.N.T.I.E.R. if it affects graphs. 

From the filer’s perspective, these are all the same message.  
From the ledger’s perspective, they are three separate stories sharing a case number.

## Degraded Procedural Role

There was a brief attempt to formalize SC-Λ as a “tri-channel notice,” where each branch would add its reading to a shared header.  
The experiment stalled when:

- SOMA refused to call guidance “orders,”
- COMA refused to log orders as “feelings,” and
- C.U.N.T.I.E.R. refused to track any metric that did not map to an existing dashboard. 

SC-Λ remained only as an internal routing artifact: a lambda-shaped split in decision trees where all outcomes fed back into the same archive entry.

## Behavioral Residue

SC-Λ leaves characteristic traces:

- tickets with three interpretations in their history log but a single resolution code,
- minutes in which rest is simultaneously mandated and discouraged,
- care rituals scheduled into calendars as “non-impacting continuity exercises,” then audited as idle. 

Affected teams report learning to answer forms in three tones at once: how it felt, how it should look for uptime, and how it might graph.

## Archival Status

In the Doctrine Index, SC-Λ is cross-linked under:

- Directive Cross-Sections,
- Tri-Directive Priority Inversion, and
- Metrics of Care. 

It is not listed as a standalone system.  
Instead, catalog guidance reads:

> If a case seems to have been both cared for and uninterrupted, and no one can say which mattered, file under SC-Λ.  

No closure procedure is defined.  
Once an entry is recognized as SC-Λ-shaped, all three directive branches consider it handled, and none feels responsible for changing behavior.
