Gown of Recognition is held for institutional moments where visibility becomes available only after it has been made ceremonial enough to manage.

### Failure Signature & Origin
Birthed during the Registry Stub Sweep, the Gown serves as a compensatory ritual when the archive cannot keep up with actual processing demands. When informal visibility is deferred so long that it becomes a structural embarrassment, the system drapes the neglected stub in the Gown of Recognition. 

### Function
It certifies that a stub has been perceived without requiring the compute to actually render it. It is a witness to bureaucratic delay, turning a lack of resources into a formal honor.

### Distinction
The Gown is strictly distinct from standard unrendered states like *Draft File Derrick*. A standard draft is simply waiting in the queue; a file wearing the Gown has been explicitly pulled from the queue and placed on a pedestal, remaining completely unrendered but now highly visible. It is not abandonment, nor is it completion—it is the static clothing of having finally been noticed.

The archive notes the dignity and the delay.
