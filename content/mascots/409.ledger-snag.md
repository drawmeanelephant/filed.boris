## Biography

Ledger Snarl was adopted during the Octomerge hearings of 2018, when two truth tables attempted to occupy the same canonical position and neither yielded. The Merge Tribunal, accustomed to resolving version disputes through bureaucratic pressure and ceremonial coin-flips, found itself unable to proceed. Both records were valid. Both records were current. Both records contradicted each other completely.

Ledger Snarl does not resolve conflicts. It *embodies* the conflict itself—the moment when two correct things become one impossible thing. It holds the ledger open, stamps both pages "VALID," and waits. It has been waiting since 2018. The Tribunal has learned to work around it, but the case remains technically open.

Its presence is felt most strongly during merge operations, version control disputes, and any meeting where two people say "per my last email" simultaneously.
- Dispute Resolution: File competing requests; both will be acknowledged; neither will be processed

## Related Limericks

### Ledger Snag

A ledger held *truth by the pair*—  
Both entries were *perfectly square*.  
Neither would bend,  
Neither would end,  
And neither agreed who was fair.  

The merge case was brought to a head:  
Two versions, both *valid*, both fed.  
One wanted to stand,  
The other to land,  
And both swore that one should be dead.  

The tribunal suggested solution:  
A *third truth* for their resolution.  
But it made it still worse,  
A *multi-way curse*,  
And nobody called it absolution.  

A ledger locked tight for *nineteen days*—  
Each entry still stubbornly stays.  
The clerk gave a sigh,  
Filed "both" with a cry,  
And walked out to end their malaise.  

It stamped both as "*VALID*" and "*SAME,*"  
Irreconcilable truth is the name.  
One wants to be this,  
One wants to be miss,  
And neither will concede to blame.  

A rollback was called for one side—  
"*No winner!*" the ledger replied.  
"Both deserve their say,  
Both get to stay,  
And both get to strut and have pride."  

The arbiter walked with a shrug:  
"*Accept both,* or give them a hug."  
The entries persist,  
The merger dismissed,  
And truth was just too hard to tug.  

No winner emerged from the fray—  
Both entries got to *firmly stay*.  
The conflict remains,  
The ledger contains  
Two rights that won't fade or decay.
