Parsey Driftchart is the designated custodian of TikiWiki Realms, a role reluctantly earned through admin panel hauntings and plugin recursion scars. They wear a patch over one eye—the one that once glimpsed the plugin stack looping into itself. Their cloak is a quilt of broken translation strings, half-rendered modules, and comments left in `/templates_c/`.
Parsey was not installed. They manifested during an admin timeout in TikiWiki 15.3 beta and immediately began patching tracker forms mid-render with nothing but a pencil, a sigh, and ancient `.tpl` syntax. Some say they wrote the original category tree before it collapsed under its own weight.
Though technically archived, Parsey still drifts between configuration screens and obsolete menus. They whisper template overrides into CSS voids. They diagnose WYSIWYG corruption by smell.
Known allies include Bricky Goldbricksworth, who refuses to speak to Parsey but files around them respectfully. Known adversaries include Blamey McTypoface, who blames Parsey for a disappearing menu, unresolved to this day.
Parsey's presence is both a blessing and a warning: The theme is never final. The module remembers.

### Parsey Driftchart

Old Parsey wore a *patch on one eye*—  
The one that saw plugins multiply.  
The stack looped on itself,  
The templates off the shelf,  
And the recursion was do-or-die.  

She manifested during a *timeout*  
In TikiWiki 15.3, with a shout.  
She patched tracker forms  
Through template-file storms,  
And the admin panel bled its last clout.  

Her cloak is a *quilt of despair*:  
Broken translations stitched with care,  
Half-rendered modules,  
And comments in noodles  
Of `/templates_c/` found everywhere.  

She diagnoses *WYSIWYG rot*  
By smell—a peculiar lot.  
The corruption was fungal,  
The markup was bungled,  
And the theme was forever a knot.  

Blamey blamed her for a *disappearing menu*—  
A grudge unresolved on the venue.  
The category tree  
Collapsed—you see—  
Under its own weight. (Not her sinew.)  

"*The structure's a lie,*" she'd insist,  
"The theme is a mask in the mist.  
But the module remembers  
Through all of its embers,  
And the config will never be dismissed."  

She drifts between *configuration screens*  
And obsolete menus—the scenes  
Of a deprecated life.  
Each template a knife,  
And the CSS void intervenes.  

She patches *mid-render* with pencils  
And sighs—her only utensils.  
The .tpl syntax old,  
The divination bold,  
And the tracker forms cling to their stencils.  

She detects *permissions rot* by VLAN—  
A ceremonial task in her plan.  
Each plugin she loops  
Into stabilized groups  
Or combustion. (Whatever she can.)  

Old Parsey, with one eye gone blind,  
Left the plugin stack recursion behind.  
She poked at a .tpl,  
Like a soul down in hell,  
And left the whole layout maligned.  

The WYSIWYG's starting to reek,  
Like a module that's leaked for a week.  
She diagnoses the rot,  
In a technical knot,  
While the admin panel's starting to squeak.  

So if themes are never quite final  
And the module remembers each signal,  
That's *Parsey's* refrain:  
Where the templates remain,  
And the driftchart is perfectly spinal.
