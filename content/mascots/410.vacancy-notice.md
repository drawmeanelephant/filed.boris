## Biography

Vacancy Notice first appeared in 2015, pinned to a door in the east wing of the Archive Rehousing facility—a door that, according to building schematics, had once led to a supply closet. The closet was no longer there. Not emptied—*absent*. The doorframe opened onto drywall that had never been cut.

The notice itself read simply: "VACANCY." No room number. No date. No forwarding address.

When the first archivist acknowledged it—said aloud, "There's a vacancy notice here"—the notice moved. Not dramatically. Just… three feet further down the hall. A second acknowledgment pushed it through the wall entirely. By the third, it had withdrawn into the building's foundation, and the Committee voted unanimously to seal the record.

Vacancy Notice is the mascot of things that are permanently gone. Not missing. Not misplaced. *Gone.* It withdraws from every attempt to confirm its existence, as if acknowledgment itself is a form of trespass.
- Forwarding Address: None. Do not ask again.
