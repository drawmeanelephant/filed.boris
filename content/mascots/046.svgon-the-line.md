## Biography

SVGon-the-Line emerged not through mere chance, but through sacred summoning. A frustrated council of web developers, exasperated by the blurry tyranny of raster graphics, turned to the SVG2 specification as holy text. Chanting its intricate elements in desperation, they invoked SVGon from the digital ether—a radiant figure of infinite crispness and contempt.
Born from `<path>` and `<defs>`, clad in scalable robes, he rose as the smug prophet of vector purity. His purpose: to purge bitmap apostasy and usher in an age of mathematical elegance. Every curve he draws is judgment. Every render, a sermon.
“Precision is the cornerstone of truth,” he intones, dismissing resolution as a myth invented by pixel heretics. He tolerates anti-aliasing only as a concession to mortal screens—never as doctrine.
Raised by spec. Feared by PNGs. Worshipped by diagram engines.

### SVGon the Line

SVGon declared with *great might*:  
"I scale beyond all meaning and sight.  
Zoom in to your god—  
I remain, unflawed,  
And the raster folk tremble with fright."  

"*JPEG's a traitor to clarity,*"  
He said with magnetic disparity.  
"PNG's polite  
But witless despite  
Being lossless." What charitable rarity.  

He crashed *raster software* from disdain—  
Just by existing on the same plane.  
His vectors were pure,  
His contempt was sure,  
And the bitmaps dissolved in the rain.  

His *golden ratio* staff he'd wield  
Across every rendering field.  
"600 DPI  
Or don't even try!"  
And the canvas with no intent was repealed.  

"*Compression is sin,*" he'd intone.  
"I do not forgive. I alone  
Am infinitely crisp,  
Not a pixel's lisp,  
And my curves are mathematically grown."  

He refused to render *beside*  
Any raster-based mascot with pride.  
He sanitized the DOM,  
Recited a psalm,  
And the bitmap was gently denied.  

*RENDER_DENIED:* resolution  
Too low for SVGon's absolution.  
ALIASING detected?  
Visual contempt ejected.  
BITMAP_COLLISION: format dissolution.  

He became *smugly luminous* when  
Exported at infinite zoom, and then  
Each path was a sermon,  
Each curve was a German  
Engineering marvel. Amen.  

"*Anti-aliasing is for cowards,*"  
He said, shining brighter than Howard's  
Best render attempt.  
"This canvas lacks contempt,"  
He sniffed, as the raster devoured.  

So if vectors reign supreme and clean  
And the scalability's perfectly keen,  
That's *SVGon's* domain:  
Where resolution's profane,  
And the DPI's the holiest scene.  

He refused raster faith as a rule,  
Declaring pixels an obsolete tool.  
No blur would be blessed,  
No aliasing confessed,  
Just curves as a doctrinal fuel.  

He scaled past the bounds of the frame,  
Till coordinates forgot his name.  
Beyond viewport edge,  
He made a soft pledge,  
To exist where no raster could claim.
