## 🧠 Biography

Octomerge manifested in the wreckage of a rebased hotfix that hit production during a Friday deployment. Its limbs represent incompatible timelines. It doesn’t resolve conflicts — it *incubates* them.
Git ancestry lost to recursive rebases. Assigned to Syncus Repolectus.
**“Please resolve conflicts before I resolve you.”**

## 🪪 Credentials

- **Species:** Spectral octopus of unresolved merges
- **Function:** Appears during persistent versioning collapse. Lives in abandoned repos, multiplies via accidental forks.
- **Last Known Good State:** Seen gliding through a deprecated GitLab instance in 2023. Left behind six detached HEADs and a sad README.
The incidents listed under relatedLorelog document how Octomerge's presence distorts reporting surfaces.

## 💡 Fun Facts

- Eight limbs, all blocked by different PRs
- Drips stale coffee onto `main`
- Can only communicate through conflicting `.diff` files
- Feeds on unresolved changelogs
- Recognizes all line endings, respects none
- Draws ASCII tentacles in commit messages
- Logs into production while whispering “force push”

## 📎 Usage Notes

Invoke Octomerge when:

- You must merge three long-lived branches with no clear base
- You need a scapegoat with limbs
- You want your Git log to look like spaghetti trapped in a fractal

### Octomerge

Octomerge emerged from a *Friday deploy*—  
A rebased hotfix that failed to enjoy  
Production's embrace.  
His tentacled face  
Just watched the whole pipeline destroy.  

He *force-pushed* over a critical fix  
While whispering git-lore—dirty tricks.  
The hotfix was gone,  
The main branch withdrawn,  
And the changelog looped in the mix.  

He rebases his own *PRs* first,  
Before reviews—that's the worst.  
The team never saw  
The diff, raw and raw,  
And the merge was recursively cursed.  

"*Please resolve conflicts before I*  
Resolve you," he'd say with a sigh.  
His tentacles spread  
Through each detached HEAD,  
And the README just wanted to cry.  

He left behind six *detached HEADs*  
In a deprecated GitLab—like threads  
Of an ancient web,  
Each commit at ebb,  
And a sad README lost in the dreads.  

He annotates *haunted commit logs*  
With ASCII tentacles, like cogs  
Of a broken machine.  
Each message obscene,  
And the history: murky as bogs.  

He feeds on *unresolved changelogs*—  
Each conflict, a feast. He just dogs  
Every spaghetti graph,  
Each fractured diff path,  
And the merge queue sinks into the fogs.  

"*Every timeline matters,*" he said,  
"Even the broken ones." (He's not dead.)  
He just incubates  
The conflicting fates,  
And the branches remain in his stead.  

So if git logs look like spaghetti  
In fractals—recursive confetti—  
That's *Octomerge's* reign:  
Where the branches abstain  
From resolving. He's always already.
