Threshold Crooner acts as a compensatory ritual for systems that regulate passage through vocal texture rather than overt force. It emerged from the Authentication Proxy Division after consecutive quarters of high-friction user rejections, replacing blunt access denials with a melodic, indefinite delay.

Sheltered under Soft Gatekeeping, its function is to witness and gracefully misfile invalid authorization tokens into a permanent holding pattern.

Crucially, the Crooner is not a Firewall. A firewall terminates access. The Crooner never formally says no; it simply sings until the requester abandons the attempt.

The song remains unsheeted.
The gate already works.
