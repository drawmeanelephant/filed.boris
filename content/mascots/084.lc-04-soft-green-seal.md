## Designation and Habitat

Birthed by the **Managed Absence Spine** during early classification experiments, LC-04 (informally the Soft Green Seal) was originally a confidence marker. It is now referenced across assurance memoranda without a stable definition. It preserves a specific systemic failure: the exact moment a complex, unresolved anomaly was simply relabeled as "acceptable" due to administrative fatigue.

## Archival Function

LC-04 operates at the archive layer; it marks records as socially settled, not dashboards as currently healthy.

The seal is cited interchangeably as completion, exemption, or preferred tone. Its presence signals that a condition felt acceptable at the time, even when no documented threshold can be found. It witnesses and misfiles the exact point where investigation was surrendered in favor of optics.

## Distinction

LC-04 governs archival comfort language. It does not claim that conditions were sufficient, only that they were once treated as acceptable to file and move past. It must be sharply distinguished from active telemetry trackers or live status sprites. While those monitor operational health, LC-04 is never a proof of function; it is entirely a proof of procedural abandonment.

Degraded Procedural Role

Originally attached to optics briefs as a confidence marker, LC-04 now persists mainly as a screenshot artifact. Teams invoke it as proof that “this used to be green” when questioned about unresolved findings. LC-04 is not applied to live graphs; it is cited after the fact when someone needs proof that the record once felt green, masking the failure to actually resolve the underlying gap.
Behavioral Residue

- Appears in training decks with captions that never quite match.  
- Surfaces in dispute threads as evidence that someone once approved something.  
- Rarely maps to any surviving configuration or rule set. 

Archival Status

Filed as a low-confidence object with mascot drift. The archive maintains LC-04 as a reminder that comfort indicators can outlive the conditions they were meant to describe. No attempt is made to standardize its meaning.
Intervals and states marked only by LC-04 must not be read as settled; they are merely documented as having stopped being argued aloud.

A simplified, crown-wearing derivative of LC-04 appears in training materials as Soft Green Sealie, and as part of the Recovered Procedural Artifacts sticker pack. These derivatives are treated as over-coherent mascots: they preserve the reassurance while discarding most of the ambiguity that made LC-04 a low-confidence object in the first place.
