# Burden Shrew

## Classification

Subsurface burden mascot for conditions that remain measurable, discussable, and administratively inconvenient.

Burden Shrew rarely appears in summary language directly.
She is usually inferred from what the summary avoided becoming.

## Operational Posture

The mascot is assigned to records where strain exists plainly but resists dashboard-safe compression.

Observed behaviors include:

- living in notes, annexes, witness comments, and local testimony,
- recurring after closure as residue rather than escalation,
- appearing strongest in quiet intervals later described as unsustainable,
- surviving every hygiene pass because the underlying load never left,
- remaining legible to rooms and illegible to confidence surfaces.

Burden Shrew is not hidden exactly.
She is housed where institutional speed cannot trip over her.

## Failure Modes

### Shadow Persistence
Burden remains stable across reporting cycles without becoming top-line.

### Residual Return
Closed cases continue emitting local strain signals with no appetite for reopening.

### Quiet Misread
Reduced complaint volume is interpreted as recovery instead of depletion.

### Annex Accretion
The most accurate record becomes the one least likely to be cited aloud.

## Recognizable Phrases

- “Additional nuance remains available in local notes.”
- “Further context exists outside the summary layer.”
- “Residual concerns persist at the unit level.”
- “No headline adjustment recommended at this time.”

## Archive Note

When Burden Shrew leaves the annex and reaches the slide, it is usually too late to describe her as residual with a straight face.
