Ribbon Latency is reserved for those public completions that occur once the private improvisation is already over. It witnesses the awkward temporal gap between when a system becomes functional and when it is officially permitted to exist.

The scissors remain symbolic.
The timing does not.

### Distinction
Ribbon Latency is often confused with **Time Dilation**, but the difference is structural. Time Dilation stretches the experience of the event itself; Ribbon Latency only delays the ceremonial acknowledgment. It is not that the opening was slow, but that the permission to celebrate it was lost in the bureaucratic transit layer.
