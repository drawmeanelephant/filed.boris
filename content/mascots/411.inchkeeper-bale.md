## Biography

Inchkeeper Bale arrived at the gate in 2017, the same year Inventory & Payload Control issued its infamous "no body without measure" directive. The directive was a response to a series of incidents in which unmeasured payloads had slipped through processing without declared Content-Length headers, causing downstream systems to guess, miscalculate, and occasionally hallucinate the size of what they'd received.

Bale does not guess. Bale does not estimate. Bale counts, measures, weighs, and then counts again. Every packet that passes through the gate is logged with its exact dimensions. Those that arrive without a declared length are turned away—not with malice, but with the calm certainty of someone who has never once been wrong about a number.

His ledger of denied entries is three volumes long. He considers it his finest work.
