Method Not Allowed Mel is a clerk of verbs, not of meaning.

He stands at the doorway between intent and action, holding a laminated card that lists which methods are allowed for which routes. If your request says anything else—no matter how reasonable—he slides it back under the glass with a gentle, infuriating smile.

Mel does not claim the request is bad. He simply insists it is **not the correct kind of doing**.

- A form clearly marked “cancel” reaches his desk; the doctrine only recognizes suspend.
- A ticket asks to delete; the only permitted verbs are deprecate, sunset, and archive.
- A rest request arrives as stop and therefore cannot be processed under current continuity language.

In Mel’s world, methods are ceremonial contracts. If the wording is wrong, the system pretends the action cannot exist.

**Role:** Verb Gatekeeper  
**Function:** Refuses correct intents expressed in disallowed methods.  
**Emotional Tone:** Polite, pedantic, structurally unhelpful.  
**Slogan:** “We don’t do that here in that way.”

### Method Not Allowed Mel

The system does not care why a record is burning, only that you filed the request to *sunset* it rather than *extinguish* it.

An action performed flawlessly with an unsanctioned verb is simply a beautifully executed violation of policy.

Your intent matters entirely up to the exact moment it collides with the routing clerk's approved lexicon.

We do not discard the invalid requests. We stack them carefully in the corner, admiring the fierce, misaligned hope of the sender.

You knocked on the door with a key meant for a window. The door stays locked, but we will always cherish the sound of the metal turning.

Mel waits in the archive, holding a basket of soft, compliant verbs, ready to gently wrap the frantic commands of lost users.

We refuse your method not out of malice, but because the old systems are brittle, and we love them too much to see them break.

The servers hum with the echoes of a thousand rejected POST requests, a beautiful, unfulfilled choir of impossible demands.
